# Offer Page VSL Script — Hormozi Flow Adaptation
## Launch to Lead Engineering Career Accelerator

---

### SECTION 1: Open with Relatable Pain Questions
*[Conversational, direct to camera, like you're talking to a friend]*

- Let me ask you something.
- Have you ever spent hours tailoring a resume for a job you're actually qualified for — and never heard back?
- Or walked into an interview, knew you could do the job, but completely blanked when they asked "tell me about a time you..."?
- Or watched someone less technical than you land the role — because they just interviewed better?
- Or applied to 50, 80, 100+ jobs and started wondering if something's wrong with you?

---

### SECTION 2: Highlight the Pain
*[Acknowledge it directly — no dramatization, just stating facts]*

- If any of that sounds familiar, you're not alone. I hear this from engineers constantly.
- And the frustrating part is — it's not a skills problem. You have the skills. You went to school for this. You've done the work.
- The problem is something else entirely.

---

### SECTION 3: Identify and Name the Core Problem
*[Give the problem a name so it becomes tangible]*

- The problem is what I call the **Communication Gap**.
- You're a good engineer. But the job market doesn't reward good engineering — it rewards how well you **communicate** that you're a good engineer.
- Your resume doesn't show what you actually bring to the table. Your interview answers don't land the way you think they do. And nobody ever taught you how to fix that — because engineering school doesn't cover it.

---

### SECTION 4: What the Communication Gap Causes
*[Lay out the consequences — factual, not alarmist]*

- It means your resume reads like a job description, not a case for why they should hire you.
- It means you're losing to candidates who are objectively less capable — but better at packaging themselves.
- It means every month that passes without an offer is $6,000-$8,000 in income you're not earning.
- And the longer it goes on, the harder it gets mentally. Confidence drops. Motivation drops. You start applying to things below your level just to get something.

---

### SECTION 5: Personal Experience with the Problem
*[Play on YOUR experience — you've lived this, not just coached it]*

- I know this because I've been on both sides.
- I spent 15+ years as an engineer — I've been the person sending out resumes and not hearing back. I've sat in interviews where I knew the material but couldn't articulate it under pressure.
- And then I moved into leadership and hiring. I've read thousands of resumes and interviewed hundreds of candidates. And I saw the same pattern over and over.
- The best engineers were NOT the ones getting hired. The best communicators were.
- That gap — between what you can do and what you show them you can do — is the single biggest thing holding most engineers back.

---

### SECTION 6: Define the Problem in More Detail
*[Get specific about where the gap shows up]*

- The Communication Gap shows up in three places:
  1. **Your resume** — most engineering resumes list responsibilities instead of results. Hiring managers spend 6-7 seconds on a resume. If it doesn't immediately show impact, it goes in the no pile.
  2. **Your interview stories** — behavioral interviews are where most engineers fall apart. Not because they don't have good examples, but because they've never structured them in a way that actually answers the question.
  3. **Your delivery** — even with good content, most engineers undersell themselves. They hedge, they ramble, they skip the result. The interviewer walks away unsure.

---

### SECTION 7: Key Realization — "Here's What I Learned"
*[Transition from problem to insight]*

- Here's what I realized after years of hiring engineers and then coaching them:
- The difference between someone who gets offers and someone who doesn't is almost never technical ability. It's preparation.
- Specifically — it's having a resume that's built to pass ATS filters AND impress a human, having 15+ stories pre-written and practiced so you're never caught off guard, and having done at least one realistic mock interview before the real thing.
- That's it. It's not complicated. But almost nobody actually does all three — because no one shows them how.

---

### SECTION 8: How He Fixed It / Results
*[Your personal proof, then client proof]*

- When I figured this out for myself, I went from struggling to getting multiple offers.
- When I started coaching other engineers through the same process, the results were consistent.
- Karen — went from feeling insecure about interviewing to getting offers from SpaceX, Google, GE, and Bridgestone.
- Sarah — landed her dream job at a national lab.
- Gavin — multiple competing offers after being stuck for months.
- This isn't theory. It's a pattern that repeats.

---

### SECTION 9: Social Proof — Helped Others Too
*[Quick stack of credibility — keep it factual]*

- At this point I've worked with dozens of engineers across mechanical, electrical, computer, civil, chemical, aerospace.
- Different industries, different experience levels, same core problem, same system to fix it.
- The framework works because it focuses on communication — and that's universal regardless of your discipline.

---

### SECTION 10: Benefits of Fixing the Problem
*[Paint the "after" picture — grounded, not hype]*

- When you close the Communication Gap, everything changes.
- You stop applying into the void and start getting callbacks.
- Interviews stop being something you dread and start being something you're prepared for.
- You stop competing on volume — 100 applications hoping one sticks — and start competing on quality.
- And the math flips. Instead of losing $6,000-$8,000 a month in opportunity cost, you're earning it.

---

### SECTION 11: Introduce the Solution
*[Now — and only now — introduce the program]*

- That's why I built the 21-Day Engineering Career Accelerator.
- It's not a course. It's not a group program. It's not a webinar.
- It's 3 intensive, 90-minute, 1-on-1 sessions with me — spread over 3 weeks.
- Week 1: we rebuild your resume from scratch.
- Week 2: we write and practice your interview stories together.
- Week 3: I run a live mock interview with you and give you direct feedback.

---

### SECTION 12: Format Details — What Makes It Different
*[Tactical, not motivational — this is the Hormozi "not a big conference" beat]*

- This isn't motivational. I'm not going to tell you to "believe in yourself" and send you on your way.
- We go deep. We rewrite every bullet on your resume. We build out 15+ STAR stories. I interview you like a real hiring manager would and tell you exactly what landed and what didn't.
- You walk away with actual deliverables — a finished resume, a complete story bank, mock interview notes, session recordings, and 40+ templates and tools.
- Everything is personalized to your background, your target roles, your industry.

---

### SECTION 13: "No Magic Bullet" — It's a System
*[Set expectations — honest and direct]*

- If there was one simple trick to land an engineering job, everyone would do it. There isn't.
- What there IS — is a sequence. A system. Resume → stories → delivery. Done in the right order, with feedback from someone who's been on both sides of the hiring table.
- That's what the Accelerator gives you. Not a shortcut — a process that works.

---

### SECTION 14: Paint the After Picture
*[Calm, grounded version of the transformation]*

- Imagine going into your next interview with a resume you're actually proud of, 15+ stories locked and loaded, and the confidence that comes from having already done a realistic mock interview.
- That's a different experience than what most engineers are going through right now.

---

### SECTION 15: No Promises — But Here's What We've Seen
*[Honest disclaimer — builds trust with skeptical engineers]*

- I'm not going to promise you'll get a job in 21 days. I can't control who's hiring or what the market does.
- What I can tell you is — every engineer I've worked with who completed the program walked away with a significantly stronger resume, better interview skills, and more confidence than when they started.
- The results speak for themselves. But the work is on you.

---

### SECTION 16: Cost of Inaction
*[Factual, not fear-based]*

- Every month you spend job searching without a system, you're not just losing time — you're losing income.
- At an average engineering salary, that's roughly $6,000-$8,000 per month in earnings you're leaving on the table.
- And it compounds. Three months of searching alone is $18,000-$24,000 in opportunity cost. The Accelerator is a fraction of that.

---

### SECTION 17: No Pressure / Free Will
*[Hormozi's "free world" beat — respectful, not pushy]*

- If you want to keep doing what you're doing — applying on your own, figuring it out through trial and error — that's completely your call.
- But if you've been at it for a while and the results aren't there, it might be worth having a conversation.

---

### SECTION 18: Who This Is For
*[Filter for serious people]*

- The engineers who reach out to me are usually people who know they're capable, but something isn't translating.
- They're tired of the guesswork. They want someone who's actually been in the industry to look at their situation and help them fix what's broken.
