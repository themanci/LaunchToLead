About Mansour Manci

Software and Systems Engineering Leader | Career Coach | Mechanical Engineer Turned Software Development Expert

I'm Not a Career Coach Who Got Lucky Once—I'm an Engineer Who Figured Out The System

Most career advice comes from people who either: (1) got incredibly lucky, or (2) never actually worked a day in engineering.

I'm neither.

I earned 4 promotions in 7 years at a Fortune 100 company—not because I'm a genius, but because I figured out the patterns. What makes leadership promote you? What do they actually look for? How do you transition disciplines without starting over?

This happened at a Fortune 100 industrial giant—one that employs more software engineers than many Silicon Valley firms, manages 500,000+ connected machines globally, and where failure literally impacts the global food supply.

I reverse-engineered it. Then I did it again. And again. And again.

My 4 pivots:

  • Mechanical to Software: Test engineer analyzing engine data → Software engineer writing embedded code
  • Individual Contributor to Agile Leadership: Software engineer → Product Owner & Scrum Master leading a team
  • IC to People Manager: Individual contributor → Manager leading 6-10 engineers building systems and machines that operate globally across diverse applications
  • Team Manager to Organization-Wide Lead: Team manager → Software Development Enablement Lead driving company-wide initiatives

Now I help other engineers do the same—with the same specific frameworks, not vague platitudes.

How I Actually Coach (Not How Most 'Gurus' Do)

I don't sell you a video course and wish you luck. That's not coaching—that's content consumption.

Here's how we actually work together:

Session Format

  • 1-on-1 video calls where we solve YOUR specific challenges
  • We don't follow a script—we address what YOU need right now
  • You leave each session with 3-5 specific actions for that week

Between Sessions

  • Email me your resume draft—I'll review it with specific feedback
  • Stuck on how to phrase something? Send it over
  • Need to prep for a tough conversation? We'll practice

Accountability

  • I'll check in on your progress (because most people need that nudge)
  • We'll adjust the strategy based on what's working
  • If something isn't working, we pivot immediately

This is hands-on partnership, not passive content.

Why I Do This (It's Not Just a Side Hustle)

I have a full-time job I genuinely enjoy. I don't need Launch to Lead financially.

So why do I spend nights and weekends coaching engineers?

Because I remember how lost I felt.

I remember being a mechanical engineer staring at software job postings, thinking, "I'll never break in."

I remember being passed over for a promotion and not understanding why—just being told to "be more visible" (whatever that means).

I remember watching peers get promoted while I stayed stuck, wondering what they knew that I didn't.

And I remember the relief—the massive relief—when someone who'd been there said, "Here's exactly what you need to do."

That's what I want to give you. Not motivation. Not platitudes. Not "believe in yourself."

I want to give you the map. The specific steps. The "if you do X, you'll likely get Y" frameworks.

Because once I had that map, everything changed. And I think you deserve that too.

I Didn't Do This Alone (And You Shouldn't Either)

Let me be crystal clear about something: I did not figure all of this out by myself.

Every pivot I made, every promotion I earned, every mistake I avoided (or learned from)—I had mentors and coaches helping me navigate the path. Not because I was incompetent. Not because I couldn't do it alone. But because having someone who's been where you want to go is a superpower, not a crutch.

The Mentors Who Shaped My Career

  • A senior software engineer who taught me how to think like a developer when I pivoted from mechanical to software
  • An agile coach who showed me the difference between managing tasks and leading people
  • A director who gave me the brutal feedback I needed to hear about my communication style
  • Peer mentors who helped me navigate politics, timing, and strategy

I'm Still Learning (And Still Being Coached)

And here's the thing: I still have coaches and mentors today. I have a leadership coach I meet with monthly. I have peers in other companies I bounce ideas off. Why? Because the moment you think you've figured it all out is the moment you stop growing.

Coaching = Leverage

Some people think getting coaching is admitting weakness. That's backwards. Coaching is leverage. It's tapping into someone else's 10, 20, 30 years of experience and compressing their lessons into YOUR growth.

Without coaching: Try → fail → learn → try again. (2-3 years)

With coaching: Try → feedback → adjust → succeed. (6-12 months)
