Hot take: Your resume isn't the problem.

Your resume is a symptom.

The real problem? You don't know how to talk about your own work.

You've been taught to be humble. Say "we" instead of "I." Downplay your contributions.

So when it's time to write a resume or answer an interview question, you freeze:

"What did I actually do?"
"Was that really my contribution?"
"I don't want to sound like I'm bragging."

Here's the truth:

If you can't articulate your impact clearly, no one else will do it for you.

Not your manager. Not the hiring algorithm. Not anyone.

The engineers who get callbacks aren't always the most qualified. They're the ones who learned how to translate their work into language that lands.

That's a skill. And it's learnable.
