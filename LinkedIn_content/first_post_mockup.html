Option 6: "5 Things I Wish I Knew..."

Educational
5 Things I Wish I Knew
Before My First Engineering Promotion
(Would have saved me 2 years)

Post Copy:

5 things I wish I knew before my first engineering promotion:

1. Your manager isn't tracking your wins
You are. Document every major project, problem solved, and impact delivered. Your promo packet writes itself if you track as you go.

2. Promotions happen before they're announced
By the time your manager says "we're considering promotions," the decisions are 80% made. Build visibility early.

3. Cross-team impact > solo technical brilliance
Solving hard problems alone gets you recognized by your team. Solving problems that help other teams gets you promoted.

4. Timing matters more than you think
Asking for a promotion right after budget cuts, reorgs, or your manager's rough quarter? Bad timing. Learn the cycle.

5. "Just work hard" is terrible advice
I know engineers who work 60-hour weeks and stay at the same level for years. Strategy > effort.

It took me 2 years to learn these lessons.

Now I help engineers skip the trial-and-error phase.
