# Accelerate Path: Problems Brainstorm

## The Journey We're Mapping

**FROM:** Stuck, overlooked, frustrated, not getting promoted (3-5+ years in same role)
**TO:** Promoted, recognized, confident, 30-50% compensation bump, leading high-impact teams

---

## The Problem Brainstorm

For each problem, we'll identify:
1. **The Problem** — What they're struggling with
2. **The Symptom** — How it shows up in their life
3. **The Pain** — Why it hurts (emotional/financial/career)
4. **The Method** — Which of our 76 methods solves it

---

# CATEGORY 1: VISIBILITY PROBLEMS

*"I'm doing great work but nobody knows about it"*

## Problem 1.1: The Invisible Contributor
**Problem:** They do excellent work but decision-makers have no idea
**Symptom:** Great performance reviews but passed over for promotions; peers who do less get recognized
**Pain:** Frustration, resentment, feeling like merit doesn't matter
**Method:** Organic Broadcasting™, The Scope Beacon™, The Recognition Boomerang™

## Problem 1.2: Heads-Down Syndrome
**Problem:** They believe keeping their head down and working hard is the path to promotion
**Symptom:** Working 60+ hours, delivering great results, but nobody above their manager knows
**Pain:** Burnout without recognition; watching others who "play politics" advance
**Method:** The "Hard Work = Reward" Fallacy™, The Visibility Pyramid (entire phase)

## Problem 1.3: Manager-Dependent Visibility
**Problem:** Their only visibility is through their direct manager
**Symptom:** If manager doesn't advocate, they're invisible; career tied to one person's opinion
**Pain:** Vulnerable if manager leaves, changes, or doesn't prioritize their promotion
**Method:** The Skip-Level Request Protocol™, The Skip-Level Meeting Playbook™, The Cross-Functional Bridge Builder™

## Problem 1.4: No Skip-Level Relationships
**Problem:** They have no relationship with their manager's manager or other senior leaders
**Symptom:** Senior leaders don't know their name; no advocates in promotion discussions
**Pain:** When promotions are discussed, no one is championing them
**Method:** The Skip-Level Request Protocol™, The Skip-Level Meeting Playbook™, The Gravity Audit™

## Problem 1.5: Cross-Functional Invisibility
**Problem:** They're only known within their immediate team
**Symptom:** Other departments have no idea who they are or what they contribute
**Pain:** Missing opportunities, limited network, no external advocates
**Method:** The Cross-Functional Bridge Builder™, The Cross-Functional Expansion Protocol™

## Problem 1.6: No Strategic Visibility Moments
**Problem:** They don't create or capitalize on moments to be visible to leadership
**Symptom:** They attend meetings but blend in; never memorable
**Pain:** Leadership can't distinguish them from anyone else
**Method:** The Strategic Question Bank™, The Alignment Signal™

## Problem 1.7: Visibility Feels Like Bragging
**Problem:** They think making their work visible is self-promotion and sleazy
**Symptom:** They actively avoid talking about their accomplishments
**Pain:** Others who self-promote advance while they stay stuck
**Method:** The "I Shouldn't Have to Self-Promote" Delusion™, Organic Broadcasting™

# CATEGORY 2: POSITIONING PROBLEMS

*"I don't know how to position myself for the next level"*

## Problem 2.1: No Clear Promotion Path
**Problem:** They don't know what's actually required to get promoted
**Symptom:** Guessing what they should be doing; unclear on expectations
**Pain:** Wasted effort on wrong things; frustration when denied
**Method:** The Gap Analysis Contract™, The Green Light Audit™

## Problem 2.2: Doing Current Job Too Well
**Problem:** They're so good at their current role they become "too valuable to promote"
**Symptom:** "We can't lose you in this role" or no one can backfill them
**Pain:** Trapped by their own competence; punished for being good
**Method:** The Role Saturation Threshold™ (85% Rule), The Gap Pioneer Framework™

## Problem 2.3: Not Operating at Next Level
**Problem:** They're waiting for the title before doing the work
**Symptom:** Still doing IC work when they should be demonstrating leadership
**Pain:** Can't prove they're ready because they've never done it
**Method:** The "I'm Just an IC" Limitation™, The Leader-Before-Title Mentality™

## Problem 2.4: Wrong Kind of Work
**Problem:** They're doing high-effort, low-visibility work
**Symptom:** Burning out on tasks that don't matter for promotion
**Pain:** Exhaustion without advancement; poor ROI on effort
**Method:** The Opportunity Filter™, The Quality Firewall™

## Problem 2.5: No Promotion Campaign
**Problem:** They think promotion happens TO them rather than being driven BY them
**Symptom:** Passively waiting for manager to bring it up
**Pain:** Years pass without progress; others who campaign get promoted
**Method:** The Continuous Campaign™, The Promotion Positioning Philosophy™

## Problem 2.6: Single-Track Career Vision
**Problem:** They only see one path (IC → Senior → Staff) and miss alternatives
**Symptom:** Stuck because they don't see the Scrum Master, Tech Lead, Manager paths
**Pain:** Limiting beliefs about what "counts" as advancement
**Method:** Career path education, The Gap Pioneer Framework™

# CATEGORY 11: IC → MANAGER TRANSITION PROBLEMS

*"I want to become a manager but don't know how to make the leap"*

## Problem 11.1: No Management Experience

**Pain:** Can't prove they can do something they've never done
**Method:** The Leader-Before-Title Mentality™

## Problem 11.2: Technical Identity Crisis
**Problem:** They define themselves by technical skills and fear losing that
**Symptom:** Reluctant to let go of coding; want to "stay technical"
**Pain:** Can't transition because they're clinging to IC identity
**Method:** The Coach Transformation™

## Problem 11.3: Don't Know Manager Job
**Problem:** They don't understand what managers actually do
**Symptom:** Think management is "telling people what to do"
**Pain:** Unprepared for reality; set up for failure
**Method:** IC → Manager content (Phase 6, Part C)

## Problem 11.4: Can't Let Go of Doing
**Problem:** They want to manage AND keep doing all the technical work
**Symptom:** Micromanaging; doing reports' jobs; burned out
**Pain:** Failing at management because still trying to be IC
**Method:** The Coach Transformation™, The Time Reallocation Protocol™

## Problem 11.5: Peer to Manager Transition
**Problem:** They don't know how to manage former peers
**Symptom:** Awkwardness; friends don't respect authority; unclear boundaries
**Pain:** Team dysfunction; failed transition
**Method:** The Relationship Recalibration™

## Problem 11.6: Don't Understand Metrics
**Problem:** They don't know how to measure team success
**Symptom:** Gut-feel management; can't prove team is delivering
**Pain:** Can't manage what you can't measure
**Method:** The Metrics Mindset™

## Problem 11.7: Can't Shield Team
**Problem:** They don't know how to protect team from pressure
**Symptom:** Passing all pressure down; stressed team; high turnover
**Pain:** Team hates them; poor results
**Method:** The Accountability Elevation™
