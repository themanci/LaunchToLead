PROPOSAL 2

Before/After Comparison

What I Believed vs. What Actually Works

Lessons from 4 promotions in 7 years

What I Believed

  • Work harder than everyone: Put in extra hours and someone will notice
  • Technical excellence is enough: Be the best engineer and promotions will follow
  • Stay in your lane: Focus only on your assigned projects
  • Wait for your turn: Managers will promote you when you're "ready"

What Actually Works

  • Work strategically: Align your efforts with business priorities and make it visible
  • Impact + Communication: Drive results AND communicate them effectively to stakeholders
  • Expand your influence: Solve problems beyond your role that matter to leadership
  • Position yourself proactively: Have the promotion conversation before the cycle
PROPOSAL 3

Common Misconceptions List

5 Lies About Getting Promoted

That kept me stuck—until I learned the truth

1

"You just need to work harder"

Hard work matters, but strategic work matters more. I learned to focus on high-visibility, high-impact projects that aligned with leadership priorities.

2

"Promotions happen during review cycles"

Wrong. Promotion decisions are made months before the formal cycle. You need to position yourself early and build momentum continuously.

3

"Your manager handles everything"

Your manager is an advocate, but YOU own the narrative. I learned to document wins, build relationships with skip-levels, and control my promotion story.

4

"Technical skills are all that matter"

Technical excellence is the baseline. Leadership, communication, and business impact separate those who advance from those who stagnate.

5

"It takes 3-5 years per level"

Only if you wait passively. With strategic positioning and measurable impact, I averaged 1.75 years per promotion across 4 levels.
