For Engineers on the Job Search

Your #OpenToWork
post is too
vague.

"Working on something interesting" means nothing to a hiring manager.
Here's what to actually say — in 5 steps.

// The Framework
01
Discipline & Specialty
Get specific about
your stack
Not: "Software Engineer"
But: "Backend · distributed
systems · data pipelines"
02
Company Type
Who do you want
to work for?
Enterprise? Mid-size?
Early-stage startup?
Lead with what you know best.
03
Problem Space
Name the problems
you want to solve
Skip "interesting challenges."
Try: "Real-time data · fintech ·
small senior team"
04
What You Bring
1–2 concrete wins,
zero buzzwords
Not "passionate team player."
But: "0→prod twice, solo
backend engineer both times"
05
How to Reach You
Remove all friction from the path to you
DM? Email? A Calendly link?
→ The easier you make it, the more people follow through.
