# LinkedIn Video Script: The Resume Leak Finder

## Target Audience
Recent engineering graduates and early-career engineers actively job searching

## Runtime Target
60–90 seconds

## Format
Problem → Proof → Reveal → CTA (screen recording of tool encouraged)

---

## HOOK (first 3 seconds — look directly at camera, confident tone)

"I've audited over 200 engineering resumes. Almost every single one has the exact same problem."

---

## SETUP (10–15 seconds)

"It's not experience. It's not GPA. It's not the school you went to.

It's that your bullets read like a job description instead of proof you can do the job.

So I built a free tool that finds this instantly — I call it the Resume Leak Finder."

---

## TRANSITION INTO DEMO

"Here's exactly how it works."

---

## Step 1: Paste one bullet

"You paste your worst bullet — the one you're least confident about. Takes 5 seconds."

---

## Step 2: It checks four things

"Every strong bullet needs four components: what you accomplished, a real metric, how you did it, and why it mattered to the business. Most bullets are missing two or three of these — and you can't see it because you wrote it."

---

## Step 3: You get a rewrite

"It doesn't just tell you what's wrong. It rewrites the bullet for you, using the same framework I use with clients one-on-one — not ChatGPT, not generic advice."

---

## CLOSE (look directly at camera)

"One weak bullet is never the real problem — it's a symptom. If one bullet is missing the framework, your other fifteen probably are too. This tool shows you exactly where the leak is."

---

## CTA (casual, after a beat)

"It's free, no signup wall to see your results. Link in the comments — go find your leak."

---

## POSTING NOTES

- Best paired with a short screen recording clip of the tool in action (paste → loading → red/green results grid → rewrite) as the video content
- Comment placement for the link keeps the post itself algorithm-friendly (LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with outbound links in the body)
- Pin comment: "Drop your worst bullet in the replies and I'll tell you what it's missing 👇"
- Good candidate for a repost 1-2 weeks later with a different bullet example
