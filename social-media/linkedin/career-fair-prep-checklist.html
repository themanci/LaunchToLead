Career Fair Prep Checklist

Most students walk into career fairs with no plan.

They wander booth to booth, wait in random lines, and leave with nothing.

Here's the prep checklist I give my clients.

𝟳 𝗱𝗮𝘆𝘀 𝗯𝗲𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗲:
→ Get the company list from your career center
→ Pick your top 5-10 targets
→ Research each one (what they do, open roles, recent news)
→ Update your resume and print 20-30 copies
→ Prepare 2-3 specific questions per company
→ Practice a 60-90 second pitch out loud

𝟮-𝟯 𝗱𝗮𝘆𝘀 𝗯𝗲𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗲:
→ Create a one-page cheat sheet (companies, roles, questions)
→ Check the venue map and plan your route
→ Apply online to your targets (many companies prefer this)
→ Prepare your outfit — business casual, no wrinkles

𝗡𝗶𝗴𝗵𝘁 𝗯𝗲𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗲:
→ Lay out your outfit
→ Pack your bag (resumes, pen, notepad, water)
→ Review your cheat sheet one more time
→ Get a full night's sleep

𝗗𝗮𝘆 𝗼𝗳:
→ Eat before you go — you'll be standing for hours
→ Arrive in the first 30 minutes (shorter lines, fresh recruiters)
→ Warm up at a backup company — don't start with your #1
→ Hit your top 5 in the first hour
→ Take notes after EVERY conversation
→ Ask for a business card or email before you walk away

One thing most people skip:

Follow up within 24 hours. A short email thanking them and referencing something specific from your conversation.

That's how a career fair turns into actual interviews.
