Read this resume bullet:

"Responsible for testing embedded software systems and reporting results to the team."

Now read this one:

"Designed and executed 47 automated test cases for the brake controller ECU using CANalyzer and Python, catching 3 critical defects before production — preventing a recall estimated at $2.4M."

Same person. Same job. Same experience.

Completely different bullet.

The first one tells me what you were assigned. Every other applicant writes this. It gets skimmed and forgotten.

The second one tells me what you actually did, how you did it, and why it mattered. That's the one that gets you the interview.

The difference comes down to four things:

→ What did you accomplish? (not what were you "responsible for")
→ How much? How many? What did it save?
→ What tools or methods did you use?
→ Why should anyone outside your team care?

Most engineers I coach have incredible experience. The problem is never what they've done — it's how they're showing it.

Your resume isn't a job description. It's a highlight reel. Write it like one.
