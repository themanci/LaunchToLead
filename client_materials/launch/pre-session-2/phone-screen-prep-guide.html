Phone Screen Prep Guide

The phone screen is your first impression. Make it count.

What Phone Screens Are Looking For

Phone screens are 15-30 minute conversations that filter candidates before investing in a full interview. They're testing 4 core things:

1

Can you communicate clearly?

Speak coherently, not rambling. Get to the point.

2

Are you interested in THIS company?

Not just any job‚Äîspecific interest in their mission.

3

Do your skills roughly match?

Basic qualification check‚Äînot deep technical assessment.

4

Are there deal breakers?

Location, salary, visa, availability, start date.

Common Phone Screen Questions

"Tell me about yourself"

FORMULA: Present ‚Üí Past ‚Üí Future

  1. Present: Current role/school and main focus
  2. Past: 1-2 relevant experiences that built your skills
  3. Future: Why THIS role excites you

Example: "I'm currently finishing my Mechanical Engineering degree at [University], focused on product design. Last summer I interned at [Company] where I redesigned a fixture process that cut changeover time by 30%. I'm excited about this role because I want to apply that design optimization mindset to [Company's] automation products."

"Why are you interested in [Company]?"

FORMULA: Company Mission + Personal Alignment

  1. What they do: Show you researched them
  2. Why it matters to you: Connect to your values/goals
  3. How you can contribute: What you'd bring

‚ö†Ô∏è TRAP: Giving a generic answer that could apply to any company. Name specific products, recent news, or initiatives.

"What are you looking for in your next role?"

FORMULA: Growth + Impact + Learning

  1. Growth: Development opportunities you want
  2. Impact: Type of problems you want to solve
  3. Tie to them: Why this role offers these things

Example: "I'm looking for a role where I can grow technically while working on meaningful problems. I want to be challenged by complex design work, and from what I've learned about your team's approach to [specific project], that's exactly the kind of environment where I thrive."
