The Problem

You finally got an offer! 🎉 But now they want an answer by Friday... and you're still waiting to hear back from your dream company.

Or worse: you have TWO offers, different deadlines, and no idea how to buy time without losing both.

Common Mistakes:

  • • Accepting the first offer out of panic (then regretting it)
  • • Asking for an extension without a good reason
  • • Telling Company A about Company B in a way that sounds like a threat
  • • Missing deadlines and losing offers entirely
📅

Script 1: Requesting a Deadline Extension

Use when you need more time to make a decision. Most companies will grant 3-7 additional days if you ask professionally.

📧 Email Script:

Subject: [Your Name] - Offer Timeline Discussion

Hi [Recruiter Name],


Thank you again for the offer to join [Company] as a [Position]. I'm genuinely excited about the opportunity and the team.


I want to make sure I'm making a thoughtful decision that's right for both of us. Would it be possible to extend the deadline to [specific date, typically 5-7 days out]? This will give me the time I need to fully evaluate and commit with confidence.


I really appreciate your understanding, and I'll be in touch well before [new date].


Best regards,
[Your Name]

💡 Pro Tips:

  • • Always give a specific new date (not "a few more days")
  • • Don't over-explain or make up reasons
  • • Express continued enthusiasm for the role
  • • One extension is normal; asking twice raises red flags
🚀

Script 2: Accelerating Another Company's Process

Use when you have an offer from Company A but are waiting on Company B (your preferred choice). This can speed up their decision.

📧 Email Script:

Subject: [Your Name] - Timeline Update

Hi [Recruiter/Hiring Manager at Company B],


I wanted to give you a quick update on my job search. I've recently received an offer from another company with a deadline of [date].


That said, [Company B] remains my top choice because of [specific reason: team, mission, role, etc.]. Is there any way to expedite the interview process? I want to make sure I can give [Company B] full consideration before making my decision.


I'm happy to accommodate any schedule that works for your team.


Thank you for your understanding,
[Your Name]

💡 Why This Works:

  • • Creates urgency without being pushy
  • • Shows you're desirable (other companies want you)
  • • Expresses genuine preference for their company
  • • Gives them a concrete deadline to work with
⚖️

Script 3: Managing Multiple Offers

Congratulations—you're in demand! Here's how to handle multiple offers professionally without burning bridges.

📞 Phone Script (to Company A when you prefer Company B):

"Hi [Name], thank you so much for the offer. I'm genuinely excited about [Company A], and I'm seriously considering it.


I want to be transparent with you—I'm also in the final stages with another company. I expect to have clarity by [date]. Would it be possible to extend my deadline to [date] so I can make the most informed decision?


I really appreciate your patience, and I'll keep you updated on my timeline."

✅ DO:

  • • Be honest about having other offers
  • • Give specific timelines
  • • Express genuine interest in both
  • • Keep all parties updated

❌ DON'T:

  • • Use offers as leverage aggressively
  • • Name the other company (unless asked)
  • • Play games or be dishonest
  • • String companies along indefinitely
🤝

Script 4: Declining an Offer Gracefully

Always decline professionally. The engineering world is small, and today's recruiter could be tomorrow's hiring manager at your dream company.

📧 Email Script:

Subject: [Your Name] - Offer Decision

Hi [Recruiter Name],


Thank you so much for the offer to join [Company] as a [Position]. After careful consideration, I've decided to accept another opportunity that aligns more closely with my career goals at this time.


This was a difficult decision—I was genuinely impressed by [specific thing: the team, the mission, the technology, etc.]. I hope our paths cross again in the future.


Thank you again for your time and consideration throughout the process.


Best regards,
[Your Name]

💡 Remember:

  • • Decline promptly—don't leave them hanging
  • • Be gracious and appreciative
  • • You don't need to explain your reasons in detail
  • • Connect on LinkedIn to maintain the relationship
📊

Quick Reference: Timeline Decision Tree

Scenario: You get an offer with a short deadline

→ Request an extension (Script 1). Most companies say yes.

Scenario: You're waiting on your dream company

→ Contact them to accelerate (Script 2). Use your deadline as leverage.

Scenario: You have multiple offers

→ Be transparent with all parties (Script 3). Try to align deadlines.

Scenario: You need to decline an offer

→ Do it promptly and graciously (Script 4). Preserve the relationship.

Scenario: They won't extend the deadline

→ Make a decision with the information you have. A bird in hand is worth two in the bush—but don't take a job you'll regret.

Key Takeaways

1. Requesting extensions is normal—don't apologize for needing time to think

2. Transparency builds trust—be honest about your timeline and situation

3. Use competing offers as leverage to accelerate other processes

4. Always decline graciously—the engineering world is smaller than you think

You have more power than you think. Companies want you to say yes.

Manage your timeline strategically! 🎯
