Why Follow-Up Matters

80%

of hiring managers say thank you notes influence their decision

Only 24%

of candidates actually send a follow-up email

This is one of the easiest ways to differentiate yourself. A thoughtful follow-up reinforces your interest and keeps you top of mind.

The Follow-Up Timeline

1

Within 24 hours: Thank You Email

Send to each interviewer. Personalize based on your conversation.

2

At timeline deadline: Check-In Email

If they said "we'll decide by Friday" and it's Friday, follow up.

3

1 week after deadline: Gentle Follow-Up

If you haven't heard anything, send a brief check-in.

4

After rejection: Thank You + Stay Connected

Maintain the relationship for future opportunities.

Check-In (After Their Timeline)

Send on or after the date they said they'd have an update.

Subject: Checking in - [Role Title] Application

Hi [Recruiter/Hiring Manager Name],

I hope you're doing well. I wanted to check in regarding the [Role Title] position. I know you mentioned decisions would be made around [date/timeframe they gave], and I'm still very enthusiastic about the opportunity.

If there's any additional information I can provide to help with the decision process, please let me know. I'm happy to assist however I can.

Thank you for the update when you have one.

Best,
[Your Name]

Gentle Follow-Up (Radio Silence)

Use when it's been a week past their timeline and you've heard nothing.

Subject: Re: [Previous email subject]

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on my previous message. I understand these processes can take time, and I'm still very interested in the [Role Title] opportunity.

Even if the timeline has shifted, I'd appreciate any update you can share. I'm flexible and happy to work around your schedule if further conversations would be helpful.

Thanks for your time,
[Your Name]

After Rejection (Graceful Response)

Why bother?

Their first choice might not work out. Other roles might open. The hiring manager might move companies. Always leave the door open.

Subject: Re: [Their rejection email subject]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for letting me know. While I'm disappointed I won't be joining the team, I genuinely appreciate the time you and the team invested in the interview process.

I remain impressed by [specific thing about the company/team you liked] and would welcome the opportunity to be considered for future roles that might be a fit.

I'd love to stay connected‚ÄîI'll send a LinkedIn request if that's okay. Wishing you and the team continued success.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

üìù Follow-Up Best Practices

Be Prompt

Send thank you emails within 24 hours. Same day is even better.

Be Specific

Reference something specific from YOUR conversation. Generic emails are forgettable.

Be Brief

5-7 sentences max. Respect their time.

Proofread

Typos in follow-ups undermine your credibility. Read it twice.

"The interview doesn't end when you leave the room. It ends when you send the follow-up."
