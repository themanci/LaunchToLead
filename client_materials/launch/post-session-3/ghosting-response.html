Why Ghosting Happens

You had a great interview. They said they'd "be in touch soon." Days turn into weeks. Nothing. Crickets. ü¶ó

First, know this: Ghosting is incredibly common and usually has nothing to do with you.

Common Reasons Companies Ghost:

  • ‚Ä¢ The role got put on hold (budget freeze, reorg)
  • ‚Ä¢ They're still interviewing other candidates
  • ‚Ä¢ The hiring manager is overwhelmed or traveling
  • ‚Ä¢ Internal politics or approval delays
  • ‚Ä¢ They made an offer to someone else and are waiting for a response
  • ‚Ä¢ Poor recruiting processes (sadly common)

What It's Usually NOT:

  • ‚Ä¢ A personal rejection of you
  • ‚Ä¢ Something you said wrong
  • ‚Ä¢ A sign that you're not qualified

The Follow-Up Timeline

Don't give up after one follow-up. Here's a strategic approach to break through the silence:

Day 0

Interview + Thank You Note

Send within 24 hours of your interview (you should already be doing this)

Day 7

First Follow-Up

Polite check-in if you haven't heard back by the expected date

Day 14

Second Follow-Up

Add value or reference something new (see scripts below)

Day 21

The "Break-Up" Email (Optional)

Gracefully close the loop if you need closure

Day 30+

Move On

Mentally file it away. Focus on other opportunities.

Follow-Up Scripts

Script 1: First Follow-Up (Day 7)

Subject: Following Up - [Position] Interview

Hi [Recruiter/Hiring Manager],


I hope you're doing well! I wanted to follow up on our conversation from [date]. I'm still very excited about the [Position] role and would love to hear any updates on the process.


Is there any additional information I can provide that would be helpful?


Thanks so much,
[Your Name]

Script 2: Second Follow-Up with Value Add (Day 14)

Subject: Still Excited About [Company] + Quick Thought

Hi [Name],


I wanted to reach out again and reiterate my interest in the [Position] role.


Since we spoke, I've been thinking more about [specific challenge they mentioned in the interview]. I actually had an idea about [brief insight or approach]. I'd love to discuss it further if you're still considering me for the role.


Please let me know if there's anything else you need from my end.


Best,
[Your Name]

üí° Reference something specific from the interview to stand out. Show you've been thinking about their problems.

Script 3: The "Break-Up" Email (Day 21 - Optional)

Subject: Closing the Loop on [Position]

Hi [Name],


I hope all is well. I wanted to follow up one last time regarding the [Position] role.


I completely understand that hiring timelines can shift, and I don't want to be a pest! If the role is no longer available or you've moved forward with other candidates, no hard feelings at all‚ÄîI'd just appreciate knowing so I can update my search.


Either way, I really enjoyed learning about [Company] and hope our paths cross again in the future.


All the best,
[Your Name]

The Mental Framework

Ghosting hurts. Here's how to think about it so it doesn't derail your job search:

‚ùå Unhelpful Thoughts

  • "They must have hated me"
  • "I said something wrong"
  • "I'm never going to find a job"
  • "What's wrong with me?"

‚úÖ Reframes

  • "This is about their process, not me"
  • "A company that ghosts may not be a great place to work"
  • "I can only control my effort, not their timeline"
  • "Every 'no' (even a silent one) gets me closer to my 'yes'"

The 72-Hour Rule:

After sending an application or finishing an interview, mentally "release" it after 72 hours. Move on emotionally even while following up professionally. Don't put all your hopes on one opportunity.

üéØ Key Takeaways

It's Not Personal

Ghosting usually reflects their process, not your worth. Don't internalize it.

Follow Up Strategically

2-3 follow-ups over 2-3 weeks is professional, not desperate. Then move on.

Add Value in Follow-Ups

Reference something specific or share an insight. Stand out from the "just checking in" crowd.

Keep Moving Forward

Never stop your search while waiting on one company. Pipeline is everything.
