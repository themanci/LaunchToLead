The Myth

"Entry-level candidates can't negotiate."

This is wrong. Most companies expect some negotiation. They rarely offer their best number first. And even if salary is fixed, many other things are negotiable.

The Truth:

Companies won't rescind an offer because you negotiated professionally. At worst, they say "no." More often, they meet you partway.

The Golden Rule of Negotiation

Always negotiate with enthusiasm for the role.

Your tone should be: "I'm really excited about this role. I'd love to make this work. Here's what would help me say yes with full enthusiasm..."

Never: "This isn't enough. I need more." That's adversarial. You want collaborative.

Salary Negotiation Scripts

Script #1: The Research-Based Counter

"Thank you so much for the offer‚ÄîI'm really excited about the opportunity to join [Company]. I've done some research on market rates for this role in [Location], and I'm seeing ranges between $X and $Y. Given my [specific skill/experience], would there be flexibility to move closer to $Z?"

When to use: When you have data that supports a higher number.

Script #2: The "Help Me Say Yes" Approach

"I'm genuinely excited about this role and I can see myself thriving here. To accept with full enthusiasm, I was hoping to see a base closer to $X. Is that something we could discuss?"

When to use: When you want to keep it simple and collaborative.

Script #3: The Competing Offer

"I appreciate the offer and [Company] is my first choice. I do have another offer at $X, and I want to be transparent with you. Is there any flexibility in the compensation to help me make this decision?"

When to use: When you genuinely have another offer. Never bluff.

Signing Bonus Scripts

Script #1: If Salary Is Fixed

"I understand the base salary is set by the pay band. Would it be possible to add a signing bonus to help bridge the gap? I've seen signing bonuses in the $X-$Y range for similar roles."

Why it works: Signing bonuses don't affect salary bands, so they're often easier to approve.

Script #2: To Cover Transition Costs

"I'm really excited to join. I do have some transition costs with the move/timing‚Äîwould a signing bonus be possible to help with that transition?"

Why it works: Framing it as a one-time transition cost makes it more justifiable.

Start Date Scripts

Script: Delay Start Date

"I'm committed to starting strong and want to wrap up my current obligations properly. Would it be possible to start on [date] instead? That would give me the time I need to transition well and hit the ground running."

Why it works: Shows professionalism and commitment to doing things right.

PTO/Vacation Scripts

Script #1: Extra Days

"I noticed the PTO is 10 days. I was hoping for something closer to 15 days‚Äîwould there be any flexibility there? Work-life balance is important to me for sustained performance."

Script #2: Pre-Planned Time Off

"I have a trip already planned for [date] that I'm hoping to honor. Would it be possible to take that time, even if it's unpaid, since I'll be new?"

Pro tip: Ask about pre-planned vacation before accepting. It's almost always accommodated.

Remote/Hybrid Work Scripts

Script: Negotiate Hybrid

"I understand the role is in-office. Would there be flexibility for a hybrid arrangement‚Äîsay, 2-3 days remote per week? I've found I do my best focused work from home while collaborating in person."

Early Performance Review Script

Script: 6-Month Review

"I understand the salary is firm for now. Would you be open to an early performance review at 6 months? If I'm exceeding expectations, I'd like the opportunity to discuss compensation at that point."

Why it works: Shows confidence in your abilities and gives you an earlier path to a raise.

When They Say "No"

Sometimes the answer is no. Here's how to respond gracefully:

Response: Graceful Acceptance

"I understand‚Äîthank you for looking into it. I really appreciate your transparency. I'm still very excited about the role and the team. Let me take a day to review everything, and I'll get back to you with my decision."

Response: Pivot to Something Else

"I understand the salary is fixed. Is there any flexibility on [signing bonus / start date / PTO / remote work] instead?"

Why it works: You might not get everything, but you might get something.
