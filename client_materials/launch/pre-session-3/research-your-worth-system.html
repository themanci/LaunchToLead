The Problem

Most people accept the first number they hear because they have no idea what they're worth.

When a recruiter asks "What are your salary expectations?", you panic. You throw out a number that feels reasonable. Maybe you aim low because you don't want to lose the opportunity. Maybe you aim high and get silence.

The Guessing Game:

No research ‚Üí Guess ‚Üí Undervalue yourself by $10-20K ‚Üí Lose $30-60K over 3 years (with raises)

Salary research takes 30 minutes. It could be worth tens of thousands of dollars.

Salary Research Resources

Use multiple sources. Each has strengths and weaknesses. Cross-reference for accuracy.

G

Glassdoor

glassdoor.com/Salaries

Company-specific salaries self-reported by employees. Great for seeing what a specific company pays.

L

Levels.fyi

levels.fyi

Best for tech. Verified compensation data including base, bonus, and stock. Shows levels across companies.

P

Payscale

payscale.com

Good for comparing by location, experience, and skills. Covers many industries.

B

Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)

bls.gov/oes

Official government data. Good for understanding ranges and industry averages by location.

LI

LinkedIn Salary

linkedin.com/salary

Based on LinkedIn member data. Can filter by company, location, and title.

H1B

H1B Salary Database

h1bdata.info

Public visa records show what companies pay for specific roles. Actual data, not self-reported.

Before any negotiation, know these three numbers:

Walk-Away Number

The minimum you need to accept the job. Below this, you walk away.

How to determine: Calculate your minimum cost of living + savings goals. This is your floor‚Äînon-negotiable.

üéØ

Target Number

What you'd be genuinely happy with. A fair market rate for your skills.

How to determine: The median (50th percentile) for your role, adjusted for location and experience.

How to determine: 75th percentile or higher. This is where you start the negotiation.

Deferral Scripts

When asked about salary expectations before you have an offer, defer:

Script #1: The Redirect

"I'm focused on finding the right fit first. Once I understand the full scope of the role and the value I can bring, I'm confident we can find a number that works for both of us."

Script #2: The Range Request

"I'd like to learn more about the role first. Could you share the budgeted range for this position? That will help me give you a more informed answer."

Script #3: The Research-Based Answer

"Based on my research, I'm seeing $X-$Y for this type of role in this market. I'm flexible and open to discussing what makes sense given the full compensation package."

üí° Why Defer?

Whoever names a number first is at a disadvantage. If you say $80K and they were willing to pay $95K, you just cost yourself $15K. Let them anchor first when possible.
