The Problem

When you have multiple offers, comparison gets emotional fast.

Company A offers $85K. Company B offers $78K but has better benefits. Company C offers $82K and is fully remote. Company A has a toxic Glassdoor review. Company B is in your dream city. How do you choose?

The Confusion Spiral:

Too many factors ‚Üí Analysis paralysis ‚Üí Decide based on what feels easiest ‚Üí Regret 6 months later

You need a systematic framework to compare offers objectively.

The Solution: Weighted Scoring

The Offer Comparison Tool‚Ñ¢ uses weighted scoring to turn subjective "feels" into objective numbers. You'll assign weights to what matters most to YOU, then score each offer.

How It Works:

  1. 1. Identify factors ‚Äî What matters to you in a job?
  2. 2. Assign weights ‚Äî How important is each factor? (1-10)
  3. 3. Score each offer ‚Äî How does each offer perform? (1-10)
  4. 4. Calculate weighted scores ‚Äî Factor weight √ó Offer score
  5. 5. Compare totals ‚Äî Highest score wins objectively

The "One Offer" Problem

What if you only have one offer? Use the same framework differently:

Compare Against Your Ideal

Before you get any offers, define your "ideal job" criteria. When an offer comes, compare it against your ideal‚Äînot against nothing.

Set a Threshold

Decide in advance: "If an offer scores above 70% of my ideal, I'll accept. Below 50%, I'll decline. Between 50-70%, I'll negotiate or wait for other options."

Consider "No Offer" as an Option

Sometimes the right choice is to keep looking. A bad first job can set your career back. Don't accept out of desperation unless you truly have no other choice.
