The Problem

"Tell me about yourself" shouldn't trigger panic‚Äîbut for most students, it does.

They either ramble for 3 minutes, listing every course they've taken, or freeze up and mumble something about "being interested in engineering."

Recruiters at career fairs talk to 50-100+ students in a few hours. They remember maybe 5.

The Forgettable Response:

"Hi, I'm a junior mechanical engineering student. I'm interested in product design. I worked on a project last semester where we designed a... um... I also have experience with SolidWorks and AutoCAD..."

The H.O.O.K. Breakdown

  • ‚Ä¢ "I spent last summer making sure 40,000 gallons of wastewater didn't end up in the river."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå "I'm a junior ME major with a 3.5 GPA."

‚úÖ Lead with your Hook first‚Äîthey'll ask about the basics.

Making It a Monologue

‚ùå Talking for 3+ minutes without stopping.

‚úÖ End with a question that invites them to talk.

Being Vague About Output

‚ùå "I worked on a team project and learned a lot."

‚úÖ Include specific numbers, outcomes, or interesting details.

‚ùå Using the exact same pitch for every company.

‚úÖ Keep H.O.O. the same, customize K (Kickoff) for each company.

Build Your Pitch

My Hook (10-15 sec)

What's the most interesting, specific, or surprising thing about you?

My Origin (15-20 sec)

Name, school, major + WHY you're passionate about this field.

My Output (25-35 sec)

One specific project/experience with quantifiable results.

My Kickoff (10-15 sec)

Question that shows interest in THEM and invites conversation.

Your 60-90 Second Pitch Checklist

Before the Event

  • Write out full H.O.O.K. pitch
  • Time yourself (aim for 60-90 seconds)
  • Practice out loud 10+ times
  • Prepare 3+ Kickoff questions for target companies

During Delivery

  • Make eye contact
  • Smile naturally
  • Pause for breath between sections
  • End on a question (not "So... yeah.")

The Key Insight

A great pitch isn't about impressing them with everything you've done. It's about saying just enough to make them want to hear more‚Äîand then letting them talk.

"Be memorable in 90 seconds. Be forgettable in 3 minutes."
