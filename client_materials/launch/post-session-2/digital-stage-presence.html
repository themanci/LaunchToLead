The Digital Stage Principle

Think of your video setup as a stage. You're the performer, and the interviewer is the audience. Lighting, audio, and framing are your production quality. The better the production, the more seriously they take you.

ELEMENT 1

üí° Lighting

The #1 factor in looking professional on camera

The Golden Rule: Light FROM the Front

Your light source should be in front of you, facing your face. Never behind you.

‚ùå Wrong: Backlit

Window behind you = your face is dark. Interviewer sees a silhouette.

‚úÖ Right: Front-lit

Face the window, or put a lamp behind your laptop pointing at your face.

üìπ Camera & Framing

How you appear in the frame matters

Camera at Eye Level

Looking down at your laptop camera makes you seem dismissive. Looking up makes you seem submissive. Eye level = equals.

‚ùå Wrong

  • ‚Ä¢ Laptop on desk, camera at chest level
  • ‚Ä¢ Looking down at camera (double chin angle)
  • ‚Ä¢ Too close (face fills entire frame)
  • ‚Ä¢ Too far (you're a tiny head)

‚úÖ Right

  • ‚Ä¢ Laptop elevated to eye level
  • ‚Ä¢ Head and shoulders visible in frame
  • ‚Ä¢ Some space above your head
  • ‚Ä¢ You're centered or slightly off-center

When you're speaking, look at the camera lens, not the interviewer's face on screen. This creates eye contact. It feels weird at first, but it makes a huge difference in how you come across.
