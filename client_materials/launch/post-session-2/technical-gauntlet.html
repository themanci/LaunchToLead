UNIVERSAL STRATEGY

How to Approach Any Technical Question

The 5-Step Technical Answer Framework

1

Clarify the Problem

"Just to make sure I understand‚Äîare we assuming...?" Ask questions before diving in. Shows you don't make assumptions.

2

State Your Approach

"My approach would be to first... then... and finally..." Map out your thinking before executing.

3

Walk Through the Solution

Think out loud. Explain each step. If you get stuck, say "I'm thinking about how to handle this part..."

4

Check Your Work

"Let me verify this makes sense..." Do a sanity check. Use order-of-magnitude estimates.

5

Discuss Trade-offs

"This solution works, but the trade-off is... An alternative would be..." Shows engineering judgment.

üí°

When You Don't Know the Answer

It's okay to not know everything. Say: "I'm not sure of the exact [formula/process], but my approach would be to [logical first step]. In practice, I would [look it up / verify with documentation]." Interviewers value intellectual honesty and problem-solving approach over memorization.

Remember

Technical interviews test how you think, not just what you know. Show your process, stay calm, and treat every question as a chance to demonstrate your engineering mindset.
