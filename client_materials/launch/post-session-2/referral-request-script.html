The Problem

Most students end informational interviews with "Thank you so much!" and never hear from that contact again.

They had a 30-minute conversation with someone who could have introduced them to 5 hiring managers, but walked away with nothing but general advice.

The issue? They don't know how to ask for a referral without sounding pushy, desperate, or transactional.

Common Mistakes:

  • Asking too directly: "Can you refer me to a job?"
  • Being too vague: "Let me know if you hear of anything"
  • Not asking at all: Walking away with zero next steps
  • Asking at the wrong time: Catching them off guard

The Solution: Strategic Referral Request

The Referral Request Script‚Ñ¢ gives you a natural, non-pushy way to convert informational interviews into warm introductions‚Äîwhile making your contact feel like a hero, not a favor machine.

The Psychology

People want to help‚Äîespecially when they've just invested 30 minutes getting to know you. The key is making it easy for them to say yes by being specific, grateful, and giving them an out.

The 3-Part Referral Framework

1

Express Genuine Appreciation

Start by acknowledging the value they've provided. This isn't flattery‚Äîit's showing you were paying attention.

"This has been incredibly helpful. The way you explained [specific insight they shared] really clarified my thinking about [specific application]."

2

Ask for a Specific Introduction

Don't ask "do you know anyone?" Instead, ask about a specific role, team, or type of person.

"Based on our conversation, I'm really excited about [specific area/role]. Is there anyone on your team or in your network who works in that space that you'd feel comfortable introducing me to?"

3

Give Them an Easy Out + Make It Easy

Remove pressure by giving them a graceful exit, then reduce friction by offering to do the work.

"Totally understand if not‚ÄîI know people get busy. But if you can think of someone, I'd be happy to send you a short blurb you could forward, so you don't have to write anything from scratch."
