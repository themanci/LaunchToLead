Block 2: Green Light Review

The Green Light Audit‚Ñ¢

üéØ Goal

Review their 14-question audit. Identify the 2-3 biggest gaps. Prioritize what to fix first. Give them a clear sense of where they stand.

Opening

  • "Walk me through your Green Light Audit. What did you check yes on? What was no?"
  • "Which 'no' answers surprised you the most?"

Prioritization Framework

High Priority Gaps (fix in Week 1):

  • No skip-level relationship ‚Üí We'll address in Block 3
  • Manager doesn't know career goals ‚Üí Echo Email fixes this
  • No visibility beyond immediate team ‚Üí Gravity Leaders addresses this

Medium Priority (fix in Week 2-3):

  • No promotion conversation in past 6 months
  • Not seen as go-to person

Watch For

  • All green: Rare. Probe deeper‚Äîare they being honest with themselves?
  • Mostly red: Normal. Reassure them this is exactly why they're here.
  • Skip-level gap: Very common. Flag it for Block 3.

Block 3: Skip-Level Strategy

The Skip-Level Request Protocol‚Ñ¢
The Skip-Level Meeting Playbook‚Ñ¢

üéØ Goal

Get them committed to requesting a skip-level meeting this week. Walk through exactly how to ask and what to say in the meeting. Remove all friction.

Review Their Current State

  • "From your Current Situation Assessment‚Äîwhat did you write about your manager's manager?"
  • "Have you ever had a 1-on-1 with them?"
  • "What's holding you back from requesting one?"

The Request Script

Coach them to send something like:

"Hi [Skip-Level Name],

I'd love to grab 20-30 minutes with you to learn more about the direction of [team/org] and get your perspective on how I can add more value. Would you have time in the next week or two?

Thanks,
[Name]"

What to Say in the Meeting

  • "Ask about their priorities. What keeps them up at night?"
  • "Share one thing you're working on that aligns with their priorities."
  • "Ask: 'What would make someone at my level really stand out to you?'"
  • "End with: 'I'd love to do this periodically if you're open to it.'"

Handle Common Objections

  • "What if my manager gets offended?" ‚Üí "Loop them in. 'Hey, I'm going to try to get some face time with [Skip]. Want to make sure you're comfortable with that.'"
  • "They're too busy." ‚Üí "Ask anyway. Let them say no. Most say yes."
  • "I don't know what to talk about." ‚Üí Use the script above. Preparation eliminates awkwardness.

Block 4: The Echo Email System

The Echo Email‚Ñ¢
Organic Broadcasting‚Ñ¢

üéØ Goal

Set up their recurring visibility system. Get them committed to sending their first Echo Email this week. Make it feel natural, not salesy.

Review What They Know

  • "Did you see the Echo Email section in the Visibility Pyramid? What was your reaction?"
  • "Have you ever sent a 'wins' email or weekly update to your manager?"

The Echo Email Framework

Weekly Email to Manager (+ CC skip-level when appropriate):

Subject: Weekly Update - [Your Name] - [Date]

Body:

  • Wins: What I accomplished this week
  • In Progress: What I'm working on
  • Blockers: What I need help with
  • Next Week: What I'm focused on

Key Coaching Points

  • "This isn't bragging. It's keeping your manager informed so they can advocate for you."
  • "Your manager probably has to report up too. You're making their job easier."
  • "Start with your manager only. Once comfortable, CC skip-level occasionally."
  • "Friday afternoon is the best time‚Äîend-of-week summary."

Set the Commitment

  • "When will you send your first Echo Email? This Friday?"
  • "What's one win from this week you can include?"

"Now let's zoom out and look at who else needs to know you exist. Let's map your Gravity Leaders..."

Block 5: Gravity Leaders Mapping

The Gravity Audit‚Ñ¢
The Cross-Functional Bridge Builder‚Ñ¢

üéØ Goal

Review the 3-5 Gravity Leaders they identified. Validate choices. Create a connection plan for at least 2 of them.

Review Their List

  • "Walk me through the 3-5 Gravity Leaders you identified. Why each one?"
  • "For each: What's your current relationship? Have you ever spoken 1-on-1?"
  • "Who on this list has the most influence over your promotion?"

Validate & Adjust

  • All same department: Push them to think cross-functionally.
  • Too junior: "These are peers. Who has actual promotion influence?"
  • Missing obvious ones: "What about the person who runs [key initiative]?"

Create Connection Plan

For each priority Gravity Leader:

  • Entry Point: How will you get on their radar? (Ask to help on a project, request a coffee chat, contribute to their initiative)
  • Value Add: What can you offer them? (Skill, insight, help with their problem)
  • Timeline: When will you make first contact?

Key Coaching Points

  • "You don't need to become best friends. You need to be known and respected."
  • "One meaningful interaction per month with each Gravity Leader is enough."
  • "Lead with value. 'How can I help?' not 'Can you promote me?'"

"Great. You've got your map. Now let's practice‚ÄîI want to make sure you feel confident actually doing this..."

Block 6: Mock Practice + Homework

üéØ Goal

Practice 1-2 scenarios to build confidence. Assign homework with clear deadlines. End with momentum.

Pick One Scenario to Practice

Scenario A: Skip-Level Request

Setup: You play the skip-level manager. Client practices the ask.

Goal: Client confidently requests a 20-30 min meeting.

Outcome: They can articulate why they want to meet without sounding needy.

Scenario B: Gravity Leader Intro

Setup: You play a Gravity Leader they want to connect with.

Goal: Client makes a warm introduction and offers value.

Outcome: They can start a relationship-building conversation naturally.

Practice Flow

  1. Set the scene (30 seconds)
  2. Client role-plays (2-3 minutes)
  3. Feedback: What worked? What to adjust? (2 minutes)
  4. If time, run it again with adjustments
