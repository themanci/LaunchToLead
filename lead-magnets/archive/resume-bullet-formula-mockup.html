The Resume Bullet Formula

Transform "job descriptions" into accomplishments that get interviews

The #1 Resume Mistake

Most resumes are filled with "job description bullets" — listing duties and responsibilities. This impresses no one. Recruiters see hundreds of these. They skim for 20 seconds and move on.

The 4-Part Formula

🎯

WHAT

The accomplishment

⚙️

HOW

How you did it

📊

METRIC

Proof it worked

💥

IMPACT

Why it mattered

Every bullet should be an accomplishment worth bragging about — not a checkbox you checked.

Before & After Examples

✗ Before (Job Description)

"Responsible for testing engine components and writing test reports"

✓ After (Accomplishment)

"Reduced engine validation cycle by 3 weeks by developing automated test scripts in MATLAB, saving $45K in lab time"

✗ Before (Job Description)

"Worked on CAD designs for mechanical assemblies"

✓ After (Accomplishment)

✗ Before (Job Description)

"Participated in code reviews and debugging sessions"
