Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Launch to Lead Engineering coaching services.

Getting Started

Launch: You're in college or within your first 2 years of your engineering career. You need help landing your first job or switching companies early in your career.

Pivot: You want to transition engineering disciplines (e.g., mechanical to software, hardware to product management, IC to leadership role). You have experience but want to change direction.

Accelerate: You're established in your current role and want to earn a promotion, become a manager, or advance to leadership. You know your field—you just need to level up.

A 15-minute conversation where we'll discuss your current situation, your goals, and whether I can help you get there. There's no sales pitch—just an honest assessment of whether Launch to Lead is a good fit for you.

Here's how I think about it: Without coaching, you might figure things out in 2-3 years through trial and error. With coaching, you could get there in 6-12 months with a proven strategy. The question isn't whether coaching is worth it—it's whether your time is worth it.

Plus, one promotion typically pays for years of coaching. If I help you get promoted 12 months faster, that's 12 extra months of higher salary.

Plus, you'll avoid the common mistakes most students make (like bland resumes or awkward interviews).

Pivot (Career Transitions)

No. I pivoted 4 times without any additional degrees. Strategic positioning beats credentials. You need to build proof points (projects, adjacent work, certifications) and position your existing experience correctly. That's what I'll teach you.

My first pivot took 8 months with the right strategy. Without a strategy, people spend 2-3 years (or give up). It depends on your target role, current experience, and how much time you can dedicate to building proof points.

I pivoted 4 times within the same company. Internal pivots are often easier because you have credibility, relationships, and inside knowledge. We'll build a strategy that leverages your current position.
