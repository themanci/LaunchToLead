02

Key Issues Identified

1

Zero metrics on the entire resume

There is not a single number, percentage, dollar amount, or quantity anywhere in the work experience. No revenue generated, no markets sized, no projects valued, no clients served with a count, no cost savings, no timelines met. Soufiane has 8+ years of international experience across 15+ countries — that's quantifiable, but none of it shows up in the bullets. Without numbers, the resume is a list of job descriptions.

2

Every bullet describes a responsibility, not a result

"Led technical and commercial support" — for how many projects? What happened? "Advised poultry producers" — and what changed because of that advice? "Developed deep understanding" — that's a personal learning, not a business outcome. Every bullet answers "what were you responsible for?" when it should answer "what changed because you were there?"

3

No dates or scope on the family business / independent projects

The second experience block has no dates at all, and the bullets are vague enough to describe almost anyone in agribusiness. "Hands-on experience in broiler and breeder production systems" tells us nothing about what was built, how large it was, what the output was, or what happened as a result. This section either needs concrete numbers and specific project outcomes, or it should be restructured into named projects with results.

4

The "Professional Profile" and "Core Areas of Expertise" sections take up half the page but add nothing

The profile paragraph and the 10-item expertise list are generic category labels — "Poultry sector market analysis," "Investment risk assessment in emerging markets." These could describe any consultant in the space. That real estate would be far more valuable filled with specific project outcomes: markets assessed, feasibility studies delivered, investments sized, systems designed. Show the work, not the category.

Your Top Action Items

1

Add a number to every single bullet

Go through each bullet and ask: "How many? How much? How big? How fast?" For the Val-Co role: how many countries did you work in? How many projects did you support? What was the total dollar value of equipment you helped specify? How many dealers did you recruit? How much revenue did your network generate? You have 8 years of international work — the numbers exist, they're just not on the page.

2

Replace the "Professional Profile" with a 3-line summary that has real numbers

Instead of "International poultry sector specialist with strong expertise in rapid market diagnostics..." — write something like: "Poultry sector consultant with 8+ years of experience across 15 countries in Africa and the Middle East. Supported $[X]M in equipment projects, built a distribution network of [X] partners from scratch, and conducted [X] market feasibility studies for hatchery and feed mill investments." Same person, completely different impression.

3

Kill the "Core Areas of Expertise" list or replace it with specific project wins

A 10-item list of capabilities ("Rapid livestock market diagnostics," "Investment risk assessment in emerging markets") reads as a list of things you claim to do. It doesn't prove anything. Either cut it entirely and use the space for more experience bullets, or replace each item with a one-line proof point: "Hatchery feasibility: Assessed [X] hatchery investments across 4 countries, totaling $[X]M in planned capital."

4

Add dates and structure to the independent projects

The "Family Business / Independent Projects" section has no dates and reads as filler. Either add real dates and convert each bullet to a specific project with outcomes — "Led the development of a [X]-bird broiler operation in Morocco (2019–2021), achieving [X]% flock survival rate and [X] kg average live weight at [X] days" — or split it into named projects like a portfolio.
