Key Issues Identified in Original Resume

1

Zero Metrics Across 15 Years

28 bullets across 7 roles and not a single number, percentage, or dollar figure. Experience at John Deere, GM, Samsung, and Motorola is packed with quantifiable outcomes — none of them were on the page.

2

Every Bullet Is a Duty Description

"Conducting field tests," "Monitoring the quality process," "Performing corrective maintenance" — these describe the job posting, not what Rodrigo accomplished. Recruiters already know what the job requires; they need to see what he achieved.

3

No Business Impact Anywhere

Not once does the resume explain why any of this work mattered. Preventing field failures on $150K machines? Saving $50K in prototype costs? Improving first pass yield? All invisible.

4

Gerund-Heavy Verb Forms

"Conducting," "Monitoring," "Performing," "Identifying," "Supporting" — these -ing forms weaken every bullet. Strong verbs like Executed, Diagnosed, Led, and Authored convey decisiveness and ownership.

5

Generic Objective Statement

"To contribute to the Technical/Engineering field with my skills and experience" could belong to any candidate. Replace with a Professional Summary packed with Rodrigo's top 3–4 headline metrics.

6

Skills Listed in Isolation

The "Summary of Qualifications" section lists tools (oscilloscope, spectrum analyzer, Vehicle Spy) without context. Skills are 10x more powerful when woven into accomplishment bullets: "using Vehicle Spy" inside a result.

7

No Dragon Slayer Context

None of the bullets paint a problem first. "On equipment valued at $150K+ per unit" creates stakes. "Across 2 production lines" shows scale. The original resume has neither — everything is flat.
