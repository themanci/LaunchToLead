University Club Partnership

Quick reference for student org resume audit + group session events

1 The Offer (What We're Giving)

10 free resume audits — first come, first serve. Club leadership shares the opportunity with their members. First 10 valid submissions get a full written audit returned via email.

1 live group session — 60-minute Zoom call, open to the full club (not just the 10 who submitted). Everyone benefits from hearing the anonymized feedback and Q&A. Scheduled ~1 week after audits are delivered so findings can inform the presentation.

Club discount — 50% off the full coaching package for any club member who wants to go further. Exclusive to attendees of this partnership.

$200 referral bonus — any club member (including the club leader) who refers someone that purchases the full-price coaching package gets $200 cash. The referrer's name and email must be cited at the time of sale.

2 Phase 1 — Resume Audit Intake

Google Form intake (create one per club)

Create a Google Form for each club partnership. Share the link with the club leader to distribute. First 10 complete submissions with a resume attached get audited.

Google Form fields

  • Full name Required
  • Email address Required
  • University Required
  • Club / organization name Required
  • Class standing — Dropdown: Freshman, Sophomore, Junior, Senior, Graduate Required
  • Major / concentration Required
  • Expected graduation date — month and year (e.g. May 2027) Required
  • What type of role are you targeting? — e.g. "mechanical engineering internship," "full-time software role," "product engineering co-op" Required
  • What are your top pain points in the job hunt right now? — Short answer / paragraph Required
  • Any specific questions you'd like covered during the group session? — Paragraph, optional
  • Upload your resume (PDF only) — File upload, PDF restriction Required

Why these fields matter:

  • Target role type — so you can evaluate if their resume is aligned to where they're actually applying
  • Graduation date — shows timeline pressure; a senior graduating in 2 months needs different advice than a sophomore exploring
  • Pain points — lets you tailor the group session to what they're actually struggling with instead of guessing
  • Pre-submitted questions — you can prep better answers and weave them into the presentation naturally

Cutoff rules

  • First 10 valid submissions (all required fields filled + PDF resume uploaded)
  • One submission per student
  • Close the form after 10 valid entries — or set a deadline (whichever comes first)

Turnaround

3–5 business days from receiving all 10 resumes. Audits returned individually via email reply.

3 Phase 2 — Group Session

Format

BlockDurationContent
Presentation 30 min Top issues found across the 10 resumes (anonymized).
Resume-specific advice: Impact Bullet Builder framework, choosing the right experiences, showcasing independent work, formatting do's and don'ts.
Q&A 30 min Open floor. Resume questions, job search, career direction — anything goes.
Drop email in chat for anyone who wants to continue the conversation.

Presentation topics to pull from

  • The Impact Bullet Builder — Action + Object + Method + Result structure
  • Picking the right experiences — not everything belongs on a resume; relevance > recency
  • Independent experiences — personal projects, self-directed work, anything that proves initiative without being told
  • Common mistakes — vague bullets, duties instead of results, missing metrics, cluttered formatting
  • The "So What?" test — every bullet should answer "why should the hiring manager care?"
  • One-page rule — why it matters for students and new grads

Logistics

  • Platform: Zoom (you host, send link to club leader to distribute)
  • Recording: ON — ask permission at start of call, note it will be shared with club afterward
  • Transcript: Generate from Zoom auto-transcript, share with club leader
  • Screen share your presentation + anonymized resume examples

4 Phase 3 — Follow Up

Day after the group session

  • Email club leader: thank-you + recording link + transcript
  • Include link to launchtolead.io/free for members who want the Impact Bullet Builder guide
  • Ask: "Would your club want to do this again next semester?"

The soft offer (say live during Q&A)

"If any of you want to go deeper on this — I work 1-on-1 with engineering students and new grads. Three sessions, 90 minutes each, we cover resume, interview prep, offer negotiation, the full picture. Because you're part of [Club Name], I'm offering 50% off the full coaching package — that's exclusive to this group. My email is in the chat if you want to talk about it. No pressure at all."

Referral bonus (mention during Q&A or follow-up email)

"Quick side note — if you know anyone outside this club who could use help with their job search, and they end up working with me, I'll send you $200 as a thank-you. Just make sure they mention your name and email when we connect so I know who sent them."

Ask for testimonial + LinkedIn shoutout

In exchange for the free workshop and audits, ask attendees (especially the 10 who received audits) to:

  • Provide a short written testimonial (2–3 sentences on what they found useful)
  • Post a LinkedIn shoutout tagging you — even a simple "Attended a great resume workshop by @Mansour" goes a long way

Frame it as: "If you found this useful, it would mean a lot if you could share a quick testimonial or a LinkedIn post about it. It helps me keep doing these for free."

6 Pre-Call Checklist (Club Leader Zoom)

Confirm club name, university, and approximate member count
Explain the 10-audit format + Google Form submission process
Agree on timeline for resume collection (give them 3–5 days)
Tentatively schedule the group session date/time
Ask permission to record the group session
Ask what their members struggle with most (tailors your content)
Share the Google Form link for resume submissions
Ask how they usually promote events (email list, GroupMe, Discord, etc.)
Mention the 50% club discount + $200 referral bonus (share details for them to pass along)
Set expectation: you'll ask for testimonials + LinkedIn shoutout if they find it valuable

! Boundaries & Guardrails

  • 10 audits max per club. Don't negotiate up — scarcity is the point.
  • PDF resumes only. Google Form enforces this — reject anything else.
  • Don't rewrite resumes for free. The audit identifies issues and gives direction. The rewrite is the paid service.
  • Don't pitch hard during the group call. The value you delivered IS the pitch. Mention the club discount once, referral bonus once, then move on.
  • 60 minutes hard stop. Stay on for stragglers if you want, but the official event is 60 minutes.
  • Referral bonus requires attribution. The referrer's name + email must be cited when closing the sale. No retroactive claims.
  • Club discount is exclusive to attendees. Don't extend it to people who didn't attend or weren't part of the club.
