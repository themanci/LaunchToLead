The Challenge

Your team has 40 years of engineering expertise, real manufacturing capabilities, and a strong product. The challenge isn't the product — it's the conversation that happens before the product gets discussed. Engineers lead with specs. Buyers decide on trust first, specs second.

💡

The Approach

LaunchToLead's engineering communication coaching combined with relationship-building frameworks from our partnership with Hansen Beck. Built for engineers who need to connect with customers as trusted partners, not salespeople.

The FAR Framework

The Fully Activated Recommendation — 3 ways to build trust:

  • Be Useful — Add value before they ask
  • Give a Boost — Make people feel like they matter
  • Provide Security — Rational (specs, data) AND emotional ("call me at 2 AM")

The Customer Journey

The sequence every buyer moves through before saying "yes":

  • ❤️ Heart — Mobilise emotional energy first
  • 🧠 Head — Then satisfy the analytical mind
  • 🤝 Hand — Fulfil every expectation — and more

Week 1 — Learning Block

Sessions 1 & 2

01

The FAR Foundation

Day 1 · 2 hours · "Why People Buy From People"

  • • Situation mapping — each person's role, goals, and customer challenges
  • • FAR framework deep-dive (Be Useful, Give a Boost, Provide Security)
  • • The engineer's trap: Needs are technical, Wants are emotional — we're in the Wants business
  • • Pre-meeting prep system & "Throwing Pebbles" — open-ended questions that make customers dream with you
02

The Art of Genuine Conversation

Day 2 · 2 hours · "Stop Pitching, Start Listening"

  • • The conversation flow — start with the person, not the product
  • • Active listening: the 2-second pause, the "echo back," and listening for what's underneath
  • • "The Customer's Brainchild" — reinforce their vision so the solution feels like their idea
  • • 3 live role-play rounds: warm buyer, all-business buyer, and the team handoff

Week 2 — Application Block

Sessions 3 & 4

03

Reading the Room & Managing the Meeting

Day 3 · 2 hours · "The Two-Act Customer Meeting"

  • • Week-break debrief — what happened in real conversations?
  • • The Two-Act Meeting: Heart first (rapport, vision) → Head + Hand second (substance, delivery)
  • • Audience adaptation — supply chain vs. VP vs. technical SME
  • • The follow-up system: 24-hour recap, 1-week value-add, ongoing touchpoints
  • • Full mock meeting simulation with group debrief

Putting It All Together

Day 4 · 2 hours · "Dress Rehearsal & Your Meeting Playbook"
