The Challenge

Your team has deep engineering expertise, real manufacturing capabilities, and a 40-year track record of technical excellence. The challenge isn't the product — it's the conversation that happens before the product ever gets discussed.

The Engineering Default

Engineers lead with specs, data, and capabilities. That's how we think. But the supply chain manager sitting across the table makes their initial decision based on trust, rapport, and gut feel — the specs are the tiebreaker, not the opener.

The Relationship Advantage

When a customer trusts you as a person — not just a vendor — they call you first, give you second chances, and recommend you to their network. That's the difference between chasing business and attracting it.

💡

The Approach

This program combines LaunchToLead's engineering communication coaching with relationship-building frameworks from our partnership with Hansen Beck, a leadership and sales training firm. The result is a program built specifically for engineers who need to connect with customers — not as salespeople, but as trusted partners.

Core Framework: The Fully Activated Recommendation (FAR)

People make decisions emotionally first, then rationalize them. The FAR framework gives your team 3 specific ways to build the trust that wins business:

1

Be Useful

Solve a problem before they ask. Share insight, make introductions, add value first.

2

Give a Boost

Make people feel like they matter. Genuine recognition. Authentic interest in who they are.

3

Provide Security

Rational (data, specs, reliability) AND emotional ("I will get out of bed for you").

The Customer Journey: Heart → Head → Hand

Alongside FAR, we'll follow Hansen Beck's 3-step Customer Journey — the sequence every buyer moves through before they say "yes." Engineers who understand this sequence stop guessing and start leading the conversation with purpose.

❤️

Heart

Mobilise the customer's emotional energy. Spark excitement and desire before facts and specs. Needs are technical — Wants are emotional. We're in the Wants business.

🧠

Head

Satisfy the customer's analytical mind. Once they're emotionally engaged, they'll ask the hard questions — and your team will be ready with substance.

🤝

Hand

Fulfil every expectation you created together — and more. Demonstrate that you can deliver, not just talk. This is where trust converts to business.

📝 Pre-Work (assigned before Day 1)

Each participant selects 1 real target prospect company and researches 1 specific person at that company. This ensures we hit the ground running on Day 1 with real scenarios — not hypotheticals.

01

The FAR Foundation

"Why People Buy From People" · Day 1 · 2 hours

15m

Opening & Situation Mapping

Each participant shares their role, what a "win" looks like in the next 6 months, and what's hardest about interfacing with potential customers. This surfaces the real pain points the program will address.

30m

The FAR Framework — Be Useful, Give a Boost, Provide Security

Full introduction to the Fully Activated Recommendation framework from our Hansen Beck partnership. We'll break down the 3 pillars and the two types of security (rational + emotional). Group discussion: Which pillar do you naturally lean on? Which feels most foreign?

30m

The Engineer's Sales Trap — Wants vs. Needs

Engineers default to rational security (specs, data) because it's what we trust. But here's the reframe: Needs are technical. Wants are emotional. We're in the Wants business. The supply chain manager decides on emotion first, then rationalizes with specs. We'll cover the "Two-Act Meeting" concept — the Heart portion (Act 1) and the Head/Hand portion (Act 2). You don't get to Act 2 if you lose Act 1.

30m

The Pre-Meeting Prep System & "Throwing Pebbles"

Every customer meeting should be prepared for. We'll build a repeatable prep process: company research (15 min), stakeholder research (10 min), and 3 open-ended questions — what Hansen Beck calls "Throwing Pebbles." The goal isn't just to gather information; it's to get the customer to dream with you — to describe their vision out loud so you become part of it from the start.

15m

Overnight Prep

Refine the 3 open-ended "Pebble" questions from today's session. Map out what "Be Useful," "Give a Boost," and "Provide Security" would look like for your pre-assigned prospect.

02

The Art of Genuine Conversation

"Stop Pitching, Start Listening" · Day 2 · 2 hours

20m

Day 1 Recap & Question Coaching

Review the refined FAR questions from overnight. Each person presents their prospect and we coach together — separating genuinely curious questions from secretly transactional ones.

30m

The Conversation Flow System & "The Customer's Brainchild"

The opening sequence: Start with the person, not the product. "How's your day going?" → "Fine." → "What does fine look like?" Keep throwing pebbles until you get open-ended answers. Find a genuine personal connection. Then use Hansen Beck's "Customer's Brainchild" technique — reinforce their vision so they feel the solution was their idea from the start. The prospect brings it back to business; you follow their lead.

20m

Active Listening Deep Dive

The 3 levels of listening: waiting to talk (most people), hearing the words (better), and listening for what's underneath (FAR level). Practical techniques: the 2-second pause, the "echo back" method, and responding to what they said — not pivoting to your agenda.

40m

Role-Play Block — 3 Rounds

Round 1: Practice the opening sequence — personal opener → connection → let them bring it back to business. Round 2: Same scenario but with a different buyer personality (friendly vs. all-business). Round 3: The team handoff scenario — how to transition from the relationship conversation to the technical deep-dive without losing the warmth. Group debrief after each round.

10m

Week Break Assignment

Over the next week, practice the opening sequence in 2-3 real conversations (colleagues, vendors, anyone). Journal what happened. Send 1 "Be Useful" touchpoint to your target prospect. Research a second prospect company for our Week 2 mock meetings. Come back ready to apply everything live.

03

Reading the Room & Managing the Meeting

"The Two-Act Customer Meeting" · Day 3 · 2 hours

15m

Week Break Debrief

How did the real-world conversations go over the past week? What surprised you? Who sent a "Be Useful" touchpoint? What was the response? This is the most important debrief of the program — real results from real practice.

40m

The Two-Act Meeting Framework (Heart → Head → Hand)

Act 1 — Heart (Emotional Energy): The supply chain manager leads. Your job is to listen, throw pebbles, and understand THEIR vision. Adapt your language to the audience — supply chain cares about reliability and lead time; VPs care about total cost of ownership. Remember: Wants before Needs. Act 2 — Head + Hand (Analytical Mind & Delivery): When the SMEs join, satisfy the analytical mind with substance, then demonstrate you can deliver. We'll cover handoff scripts, visual aids (spec sheets, process flows), and how the buyer's psychology shifts from "reasons to buy" to "excuses not to buy" as real money enters the picture.

20m

The Follow-Up System

The meeting is 20% of the relationship. Within 24 hours: recap email referencing something personal (boost), attach requested technical data (rational security), include your cell number (emotional security). Within 1 week: send something useful. Ongoing: monthly touchpoints that maintain the relationship independently of transactions.

40m

Full Mock Meeting Simulation

Complete simulation of a customer meeting from cold open to close. I'll play the supply chain manager and technical SME. Your team runs the full meeting: rapport building → business discussion → technical handoff → close with next steps. Full group debrief with specific feedback on each person's performance.

5m

Overnight Prep

Build a pre-meeting prep packet for a REAL upcoming prospect using everything from Days 1-3. Prepare 3-5 visual aids for technical discussions. Draft a follow-up email template the team can customize after meetings.

04

Putting It All Together

"Live Dress Rehearsal & Your Meeting Playbook" · Day 4 · 2 hours

15m

Homework Review

Review each person's real prospect prep packet, visual aids, and follow-up email drafts. Coach on tone, personalization, and FAR balance.

45m

Full Dress Rehearsal

The most realistic simulation of the program — using a REAL prospect the team is planning to approach. The full team participates with assigned roles: relationship lead, business support, and technical closer. Complete run-through from cold open to close, followed by detailed group debrief with specific feedback for each person.

30m

Building Your Meeting Playbook

Together, we'll create a 1-page reusable checklist the team runs before every customer meeting — covering pre-meeting prep (48 hrs before), during-meeting prompts (opening, listening, handoff, close), and post-meeting follow-up (within 24 hrs). This becomes your team's Standard Operating Procedure for business development.

20m

30-60-90 Day Habit Plan & Close

First 30 days: Apply the playbook to 2-3 real prospect meetings and debrief internally. First 60 days: Each team member sends 1 "Be Useful" touchpoint per week. First 90 days: Review results — how many meetings converted? Where is the bottleneck now? Each person states their ONE commitment for how they'll approach the next customer meeting differently.
