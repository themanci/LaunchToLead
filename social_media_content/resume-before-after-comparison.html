The 7 Patterns That Took This Resume From 52 to 100

Same person. Same experience. Same 7 years. The only thing that changed was the framework applied to the writing.

What NOT To Do

✖

“Work with” / “Assist” / “Support”

Makes you invisible. Sounds like you watched someone else do it.

✖

Bullets with zero metrics

“Improved developer experience” = by how much? for how many? worth what?

✖

Describing scope without consequence

“$2M fleet” means nothing if you don’t say what happens when a machine doesn’t arrive.

✖

Ending on the technical fix

“Fixed the CAN message” — nobody outside your team knows what that means.

✖

Hiding amazing numbers

“33,000 to 25 milliseconds” buried in a paragraph vs. “1,320x improvement” leading the bullet.

✖

Quality cliff between roles

Supervisor bullets = strong. Drivetrain bullets “Supported code development” = Doer.

What To Do Instead

✔

Lead with decisive verbs

Eliminated, Built, Designed, Drove, Resolved, Achieved, Led, Orchestrated.

✔

Quantify every bullet — %, $, count, or time

“85% adoption across 2,000 developers within 4 months” = undeniable scale.

✔

Dragon Slayer: show what was at risk

“Preventing a fleet-wide safety recall” turns a code fix into a career-defining moment.

✔

Answer “Why does the CEO care?”

$120K in OT savings. $18K/month in warranty costs. $5M warehouse hold. Revenue protected.

✔

Frame the number to maximize impact

Not “33,000 to 25ms” — say “1,320x improvement.” Make the math hit harder.

✔

Uniform quality: cut weak bullets, don’t keep them

3 strong bullets > 9 bullets where 4 are filler. Quality over quantity.
