Verbatim Script - Say This Exactly

SCENE 1: THE CLICHE (0-5s)

Stop telling interviewers this story: "My team did nothing and I did all the work and we got an A because of me." This is the most cliche answer in every interview.

SCENE 2: THE PROBLEM (5-12s)

Here's the problem. Even if it's true, trashing your teammates never makes you look good. And just saying "I told them we need to do this" is not impressive. That's not leadership. That's complaining with a suggestion.

SCENE 3: LEVEL UP (12-20s)

Instead, flip the story. Same situation, better framing: "The team was struggling to stay aligned, so I stepped up to create structure and get everyone contributing." Now you sound like a leader, not a complainer.

SCENE 4: REAL LEADERSHIP (20-30s)

Then tell them what you actually did. Did you break the project into tasks? Assign work based on people's strengths? Set up check-ins to track progress? Hold people accountable? Help struggling teammates get unstuck? That's leadership. That's what they want to hear.

SCENE 5: THE PUNCHLINE (30-40s)

Don't tell me your team was lazy and you were Superman. Tell me how you took a struggling team and made them effective. That's not a story about them failing. That's a story about you leading. And that's what gets you hired.
