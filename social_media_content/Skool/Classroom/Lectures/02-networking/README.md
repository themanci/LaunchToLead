# Section 2: Networking Lectures (5 Methods + 1 Bonus)

*Source: The Launch Method V3 - Section 2*

## Videos to Record

### 2.1 The Warm Intro Protocol™ ⭐ PRIORITY
- **Length:** 8-10 min
- **Status:** Not recorded
- **Key Points:**
  - Build strategic connections through existing network
  - Script: "Hi [Name], I'm reaching out because I'm transitioning into [field] and I noticed you work at [Company]. I'd love to learn about your experience there. Would you be open to a 15-minute chat?"
  - Focus on learning, not asking for jobs directly
  - Follow up protocol and relationship maintenance

### 2.2 The Resource Activation System™ ⭐ PRIORITY
- **Length:** 5-7 min
- **Status:** Not recorded
- **Key Points:**
  - Map your existing network (professors, advisors, classmates, family friends)
  - Categories: Academic, Professional, Personal, Alumni
  - Create outreach list prioritized by connection strength
  - Activate dormant connections appropriately

### 2.3 The Networking Mindset Shift™
- **Length:** 5 min
- **Status:** Not recorded
- **Key Points:**
  - Networking is relationship building, not transaction
  - Focus on mutual value exchange
  - Long-term relationship cultivation over quick asks
  - Reframe from "using people" to "building community"

### 2.4 The Referral Request Script™
- **Length:** 5-7 min
- **Status:** Not recorded
- **Key Points:**
  - When and how to ask for internal referrals
  - Script templates for different relationship levels
  - Making it easy for the referrer (provide resume, job link, talking points)
  - Following up and expressing gratitude

### 2.5 The Career Center Activation Checklist™
- **Length:** 5-7 min
- **Status:** Not recorded
- **Key Points:**
  - Services available: resume review, mock interviews, job postings
  - Alumni network access and events
  - On-campus recruiting opportunities
  - Building relationship with career counselors

### 2.6 BONUS: The Company Research Deep Dive™
- **Length:** 5-7 min
- **Status:** Not recorded
- **Key Points:**
  - Research company before networking conversations
  - Understanding company culture, recent news, products
  - Finding common ground with connections
  - Demonstrating genuine interest
