# Don't Reveal Deal Breakers Before You Have an Offer

## The Mistake I Keep Seeing

I keep seeing engineers shoot themselves in the foot during interviews.

They bring up deal breakers before they even have an offer:

> "I'm not sure I'd relocate."
> "I really prefer remote."
> "That salary range might be too low."
> "I don't love that location."

Every time I hear this, I cringe. You're killing your leverage.

---

## Why This Hurts You

Before you have an offer, you're **competing**. You're being compared to other candidates. You're being evaluated not just on skills, but on "fit" and "ease of hire."

When you start listing reasons you might say no, you're planting seeds in their mind:

| What You Say | What They Think |
|--------------|-----------------|
| "I'm not sure about relocating" | "This person has baggage" |
| "That salary is probably too low" | "The other candidate didn't mention this" |
| "I really need fully remote" | "Why take the risk on this one?" |

You're giving them reasons to pick someone else.
