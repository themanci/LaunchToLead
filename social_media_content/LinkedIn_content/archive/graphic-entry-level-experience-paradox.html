4 Ways to Build Experience Before You Graduate

1

Highlight What You Already Have

Internships, coursework projects, capstone, lab work — this all counts. Stop underselling yourself.

2

Build Independent Projects

Work on something not assigned by a professor. Shows passion, initiative, and self-motivation.

3

Consider a Co-Op

Delay graduation 1 semester for real experience. Tiny cost now, decades of benefit later.

4

Choose Relevant Part-Time Work

TA, tutor, lab assistant, local firm — even if it pays less. It's an investment in your resume.

The experience gap is real — but it's not insurmountable.
