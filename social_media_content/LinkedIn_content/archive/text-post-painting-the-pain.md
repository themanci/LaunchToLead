# LinkedIn Post: Painting the Pain Framework

## Post Type
Text Post (Interview Storytelling)

## Topic
Master the "Painting the Pain" Framework for STAR Method Stories

---

## POST CONTENT

Most candidates rush through their interview stories.

They speed through the situation like it's a boring intro.
Then wonder why their "amazing results" don't land.

Here's the truth:

**The bigger the monster you slay, the bigger the hero you become.**

I call this "Painting the Pain."

Instead of: "We had a project running behind schedule..."

Try this:
‚Ü≥ "Our flagship product launch was 6 weeks behind"
‚Ü≥ "The CEO was personally reviewing our progress daily"
‚Ü≥ "Every week of delay cost us $200K in market opportunity"
‚Ü≥ "Two team leads had already quit from the pressure"

See the difference?

Same situation. Completely different weight.

Here's how to "Paint the Pain" in your STAR stories:

**1. Quantify the Stakes**
‚Üí Revenue at risk
‚Üí Customers affected
‚Üí Team members impacted
‚Üí Timeline pressure

**2. Show the Tension**
‚Üí Who was watching?
‚Üí What was the consequence of failure?
‚Üí Why couldn't the "obvious" solutions work?

**3. Create the Contrast**
‚Üí The bigger the problem you describe
‚Üí The more impressive your solution becomes
‚Üí Without changing a single fact

Most people spend 10% of their story on the situation.

Top performers spend 40%.

They let the interviewer *feel* the weight of the problem before revealing how they solved it.

That's what makes them memorable.

---

Try this in your next interview:

Before you explain what you DID...
Make them understand why it MATTERED.
