Low GPA?

How to Own It and Still Land the Job

A low GPA isn't a death sentence. It's a chapter in your story—not the whole book.

The A-B-C Response Framework

A

Acknowledge: "My GPA is 2.6. I struggled early on due to personal circumstances."

B

Bridge: "But I've made significant improvements since then."

C

Capability: "I raised my major GPA, earned A's in my hardest courses, and gained hands-on experience."

1

Always include your GPA

Leaving it off makes recruiters assume the worst. Use your major GPA if it's higher.

2

Show the upward trajectory

1.7 → 2.6 is a story of grit. A's in your hardest classes prove capability.

3

Shift focus to experience

Internships, projects, and leadership matter more. Only 12% of STEM jobs have strict GPA cutoffs.

4

GPA fades fast

After your first job, no one cares. One year of experience eclipses four years of grades.

Own it. Explain it. Show your growth.
