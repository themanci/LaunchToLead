## The Missing Piece

When you finish your STAR story at "R" (Results), you've told what happened. But you haven't answered the question the interviewer is secretly asking:

"Did you actually LEARN from this?"

That's where most candidates get stuck. They deliver a polished story, but then they're caught off-guard by the most obvious follow-up question.

---

## Here's What Actually Works

**Add an L to your STAR.**

**S** = Situation  
**T** = Task  
**A** = Action  
**R** = Results  
**L** = Lessons Learned (What I'd Do Differently)

When you end your story with "Here's what I learned" or "Next time, I would approach it by doing X instead," you've just told the interviewer:

â†’ You reflect on your work  
â†’ You're not arrogant (you admit gaps)  
â†’ You're coachable  
â†’ You actually care about improvement  

These are the people companies WANT to hire.

---

## How to Execute This

**Step 1: Document the full story**

Write out the entire narrative in 2-3 paragraphs. Include dates, names, specific numbers, what you were thinking, what you learned. Extreme detail.

**Step 2: Extract the bullets**

Boil that down to 5-7 bullet points you can memorize and deliver conversationally in ~2 minutes.

**Step 3: Practice obsessively**

Not robotic. Natural. Like you're telling a friend what happened. Practice until you can deliver it while thinking about something else (that's how you know it's truly internalized).

**Step 4: When you get the follow-up**

"What would you do differently?"

You already know the answer. You practiced it. You own it.
