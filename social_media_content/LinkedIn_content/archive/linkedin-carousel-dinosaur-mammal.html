Are you working for a Dinosaur or a Mammal?

Why knowing the difference is the most underrated career skill in engineering.

1 / 8

Imagine joining the Galactic Empire then complaining there's too much protocol to fly a TIE Fighter.

If you wanted to be scrappy, you should've joined the Rebellion.

2 / 8

The problem isn't the company.

It's your lack of environmental awareness.

In the corporate "Circle of Life", you're either working for a Dinosaur or a Mammal. Both exist. Both play by different rules.

3 / 8

The Enterprise

The Dinosaur.

Strength

Massive scale. Turn a bolt here, it affects millions of lives. Resources to solve "impossible" problems.

Constraint

"Neural lag." Decisions take time because the skeleton is heavy and the stakes are high.

Advice

Don't make it "scurry." Direct its massive power correctly. Play to stability.

4 / 8

The Startup

The Mammal.

Strength

Speed and mutation. Pivot your entire strategy in a single afternoon.

Constraint

Vulnerability. No safety net. No 50-year legacy to lean on.

Advice

Don't complain about lack of "process." You are the process. Play to agility.

5 / 8

Know Your Habitat

You can nudge — but don't fight its nature.

Dinosaur

Push it to be 5% more nimble.

But know its limits. It will never scurry.

Mammal

Push it to be more organized.

But don't stifle its speed. It will never stomp.

Success rewards the Mammal by turning it into a Dinosaur — too large to stomp out the smaller competitors now nibbling at its feet.

6 / 8

If you get the chance — try both.

Only ever at a Dinosaur?
You'll forget how to move fast.

Only ever at a Mammal?
You won't understand how to scale.

The best engineers understand the organism — and play to its strengths.

7 / 8

Stop complaining that a T-Rex can't hide in a burrow.

Understand the organism.
Play to its strengths.
