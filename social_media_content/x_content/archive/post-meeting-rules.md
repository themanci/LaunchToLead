**Post 2:**
1. Send a clear agenda 24 hours ahead.

Not "let's discuss."

What's the goal? What decision? What info do we need?

**Post 3:**
2. Actually facilitate.

Call on people directly. "Sarah, what's your take?"

No more hiding in the corner while decisions happen without you.

**Post 4:**
3. Put away the laptops.

Context-switching kills 40% of your focus. If you can't be present, decline the meeting.

**Post 5:**
4. End with actions.

"Who's doing what by when?"

Follow up in writing. This alone cut my follow-up meetings in half.

**Post 6:**
Try one of these in your next meeting.
