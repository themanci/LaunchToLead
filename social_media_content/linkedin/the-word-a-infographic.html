The Word Everyone Misses
There's one word in every behavioral interview question
that most people completely ignore.
The Question
"Tell me about A time you…"
A = one story, one incident, one moment.
Not a pattern. Not a habit. Not "usually" or "sometimes."
✗ A Pattern
What most people say
"Sometimes customers get upset and I help calm them down. Usually I just try to de-escalate the situation and make sure they leave happy."
Vague. Generic. Forgettable.
✓ A Moment
What you should say
"One time, a woman placed a huge catering order for a birthday party. We completely missed it. She showed up at 6pm — nothing was ready. She called my manager on the spot."
Specific. Visual. Unforgettable.
Before your next interview — ask yourself: "Am I describing a pattern, or a moment?"
