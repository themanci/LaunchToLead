5 RULES TO KEEP YOUR RATIO HIGH
1 Under-Promise. Over-Deliver.
Think Friday? Say Monday. Deliver Friday.
"Exceeded expectations" — without extra work.
▸ Manage the denominator.
2 Guard Your "Yes"
Every "I'll take that on" adds to your denominator.
If it's not a priority — don't volunteer.
▸ Saying no to the wrong things = saying yes to the right things.
3 Meetings = Promises
You forgot. Your manager didn't.
Every casual "I'll handle it" that doesn't get handled chips away at credibility.
▸ Every word in a meeting is a commitment.
4 Track Your Commitments
If it came out of your mouth, write it down.
Notebook, Notion, sticky note — doesn't matter.
▸ High ratio engineers have a system. Low ones apologize.
5 Protect the Ratio at All Costs
Your reputation for reliability is the most valuable asset you own as a new engineer. It's built in your first 90 days and follows you for years.
It's not about saying more.
It's about doing everything you say.
