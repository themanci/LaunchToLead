# Content Creation Guide — Launch to Lead (L²)

You are writing marketing content for **Launch to Lead (L²)**, an engineering career coaching business run by Mansour — an engineer with 10 years of engineering career experience (including 8 at John Deere), 4 promotions, and 4 career pivots. He's been on both sides of the hiring table (applied AND hired).

---

## Target Avatars

Always know which avatar you're writing for:

| Avatar | Profile | Core Pain | Emotional State |
|--------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Graduating Soon** | Engineering student, 0-1 years to graduation | No job lined up, can't turn coursework into resume content | Anxious, watching classmates get offers |
| **Recent Grad** | 0-1 year post-graduation | Sent 100+ applications, zero callbacks, losing ~$7K/month | Frustrated, defeated, dodging "how's the job hunt?" |
| **Early Engineer** (1-3 yr) | Employed, 1-3 years experience | Stuck in role, resume hasn't been updated since college | Frustrated, invisible, underpaid |
| **Stuck Mid-Career** (future) | 5+ years, 2-3+ without promotion | Watching less-skilled peers advance, invisible to leadership | Resentful, considering leaving |
> **Primary content target: The Recent Grad.** When writing a social post, email, or short-form content, default to this person. Write every piece as if talking directly to them. Use "you." Address their specific situation, not a generalized job seeker.

## Hormozi Value Equation (Use for ALL Persuasive Copy)

$$\text{Value} = \frac{\text{Dream Outcome} \times \text{Perceived Likelihood}}{\text{Time Delay} \times \text{Effort}}$$

- **Maximize Dream Outcome:** "Land a Top-Tier, High Salary Engineering Job"
- **Maximize Perceived Likelihood:** Testimonials, coach credentials, guarantee
- **Minimize Time Delay:** "4 weeks," "4 sessions," "less than 5 hours total"
- **Minimize Effort:** "We do it WITH you," "1-on-1," "structured program"

## Voice Rules

### DO
- Speak as an engineer, not as HR/recruiter
- Use specific numbers and concrete examples
- Reference real outcomes (Karen → SpaceX/Google/GE/Bridgestone, Sarah → $106K at Ford)
- Be direct about the problem and the cost of inaction ($7K/month)
- Use "you" — address the reader directly

### DON'T
- Use hype language or exclamation marks in professional content
- Promise guaranteed job placement (we guarantee the deliverables + confidence, not the job itself)
- Use salesy buzzwords: "game-changer," "unlock your potential," "secret sauce," "hack"
- Sound like a recruiter or HR professional
- Use generic coaching language — always be engineering-specific

## The 5-Part Content Framework

Every piece of content should follow this structure. This is the methodology — not just a checklist.

### 1. Draw a Line in the Sand (Old Way vs. New Way)

Every piece of content is built around one clear shift — from an old, broken approach to a better one. Determined per post based on the topic.

- **Old way:** what the Recent Grad is currently doing or believing that isn't working
- **New way:** the correct approach, your methodology, the reframe
- For short-form: one clean transition is enough. For longer content: build sub-shifts for each key point.

> "Most engineers applying right now are doing X. Here's why that's the problem — and what actually works."

Every piece of content should have an identifiable old/new contrast. This is not optional.

---

### 2. Stretch the Gap

Create desire-based tension by making the reader feel the distance between where they are and where they want to be.

**Three Levers:**

| Lever | L² Application |
|---|---|
| **Results** | Land their first career-launching engineering role — real salary, real title, real company |
| **Reality** | Rejection emails piling up, no callbacks, watching classmates get hired, losing ~$7K/month |
| **Roadblocks** | Can't package experience, don't know what counts, can't interview well, don't know what hiring managers want |

**Four Tools to stretch the gap:**

**Models** — use a simple visual or framework to help them self-diagnose. Show levels (e.g., 4 resume states from "getting filtered immediately" to "getting consistent callbacks"). Goal: get them to place themselves and feel the gap.

**Metrics** — give them a way to score themselves. Use a 4-point scale — forces a binary, no fence-sitting at "5 out of 10." Avoid open-ended 1–10 scales.
> "Where does your resume sit? 1 = getting filtered before a human sees it. 4 = consistently landing interviews."

**Metaphors** — make the gap visceral.
> "Your resume reads like a job description, not an achievement record. Hiring managers don't care what your job was — they care what you did in it."

**Questions** — ask them to self-assess across the three levers:
1. "If your resume was actually working, what would be different right now?" *(Results)*
2. "How many roles have you applied to where you were qualified but never heard back?" *(Reality)*
3. "What specifically do you think is in the way?" *(Roadblocks)*

Questions don't need to be stacked back-to-back. Load one into any piece of content and let it do the work.

---

### 3. Product Placement

Embed a natural reference to the relevant offer **inside** the content before the CTA hits. The reader encounters the offer mid-content as context, not at the end as a pitch.

**Rule:** Never interrupt content to sell. Find a genuine teaching tie-in.

**Comment RESUME → Impact Bullet Builder**
Mid-post, use a real before/after bullet example and attribute it to the Impact Bullet Builder by name. Don't explain what the IBB is — just use it as the source of the example.
> *"Here's a bullet I rewrote using the Impact Bullet Builder — [before] → [after]. That's the difference between getting filtered out and getting a callback."*

**Comment COACH → Full Coaching Program**
Mid-post, briefly reference that the result came from the 1-on-1 coaching process. One sentence.
> *"Sarah didn't figure this out alone — we worked through it together in her first coaching session, and she had a $106K offer from Ford within weeks."*

**launchtolead.io/coaching → Full Offer**
Mid-post, reference what the full coaching engagement covers — not as a list, just a passing mention.
> *"This is one piece of what we cover in the full coaching program — resume, interview prep, and the full search strategy, start to offer."*

**The Marjorie Rule:** Make the next step specific and vivid enough that the reader can picture themselves doing it before you ask. Specificity creates imagination. Imagination drives action.

---

### 4. The Easy Next Yes (The Invitation)

The CTA is an invitation, not a close. Match it to where the reader is in their awareness.

| Temperature | Who They Are | CTA |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold** — just found you, skeptical | Scrolling past, no prior exposure | Comment **RESUME** → get the Impact Bullet Builder guide (free, low friction) |
| **Warm** — been following you | Engaged, considering | Comment **COACH** → get a coaching program overview |
| **Hot** — ready to buy | Self-qualified, price-aware | Direct link → "See If You're a Fit" at launchtolead.io/coaching |

One piece of content can address all three temperatures with tiered CTAs stacked at the end.

---

### 5. Teach AND Sell Simultaneously

Every piece of content has two jobs: teach and sell. Not teach, *then* sell — simultaneously.

- While teaching: show the reader what to want and set up the offer
- While selling: make it feel valuable, because it is
- If the content is built correctly, the CTA at the end is just an invitation — not a pitch

**If you find yourself shifting gears from "content mode" to "sales mode," the content wasn't built right.**
