# Launch to Lead (L²) — Content Creation Guide (v4)

---

## Who We Are

**Launch to Lead (L²)** is a 1-on-1 engineering career coaching business run by Mansour — 8 years at John Deere, 4 promotions, 4 career pivots, and experience on both sides of the hiring table (applied AND hired). That dual-side credibility is the authority foundation for all content.

## Who We're Talking To

**One avatar. All content is written for this person.**

**Avatar: Recent engineering graduates struggling to land their first full-time engineering job.**

| | Recent Grad |
|---|---|
| **Profile** | 0–1 year post-graduation |
| **Core Pain** | Sent 100+ applications, zero callbacks, losing ~$7K/month in opportunity cost |
| **Emotional State** | Frustrated, defeated, dodging "how's the job hunt going?" |
| **What They Want** | Land their first career-launching engineering role |
| **Their Reality** | Constant rejection emails, no interviews, no offers |
| **Their Roadblocks** | Don't know how to package or communicate their experience, can't identify what counts as good experience, don't know how to present themselves, can't perform in interviews, don't understand what hiring managers are actually looking for |

Write every piece of content as if you're talking directly to this person. Use "you." Address their specific situation, not a generalized job seeker.

---

## Core Content Principle: Teach AND Sell Simultaneously

Every piece of content has two jobs: teach and sell. Not teach, *then* sell — simultaneously.

- While teaching: show the reader what to want and set up the offer
- While selling: make it feel valuable, because it is
- If the content is built correctly, the CTA at the end is just an invitation — not a pitch

**If you find yourself shifting gears from "content mode" to "sales mode," the content wasn't built right.**

## The 5-Part Content Framework (Strategy Layer)

Use this to decide *what* the content is and *why* it works, before you write a word. Section "Content Recipe" below is the formatting layer that turns this strategy into an actual draft.

### 1. Draw a Line in the Sand (Old Way vs. New Way)

Every piece of content is built around one clear shift — from an old, broken approach to a better one. This is determined per post based on the topic. There is no single universal contrast; each piece of content defines its own.

**This is mandatory for every post, regardless of angle** (see Content Angles below — even Problem, Proof, and Plan-angled posts need an identifiable old/new contrast somewhere in the piece).

- **Old way:** what the Recent Grad is currently doing or believing that isn't working
- **New way:** the correct approach, your methodology, the reframe
- For short-form content: one clean transition is enough
- For longer content: build sub-shifts for each key point

> "Most engineers applying right now are doing X. Here's why that's the problem — and what actually works."

---

### 2. Stretch the Gap

Create desire-based tension by making the reader feel the distance between where they are and where they want to be. Use the three levers and the four tools below.

**Three Levers — for the Recent Grad avatar:**

| Lever | L² Application |
|---|---|
| **Results** | Land their first career-launching engineering role — real salary, real title, real company |
| **Reality** | Rejection emails piling up, no callbacks, watching classmates get hired, losing ~$7K/month |
| **Roadblocks** | Can't package experience, don't know what counts, can't interview well, don't know what hiring managers want |

**Four Tools to stretch the gap inside content:**

**Models** — use a simple visual or framework to help them self-diagnose
- Show levels (e.g., 4 resume states from "getting filtered immediately" to "getting consistent callbacks")
- Collapse it to a binary: good enough or not good enough
- Goal: get them to place themselves and feel the gap

**Metrics** — give them a way to score themselves
- Preferred: Mike's 4-point scale — forces a binary, no fence-sitting at "5 out of 10"
- Example: "Where does your resume sit right now? 1 = getting filtered before a human sees it. 4 = consistently landing interviews."
- Avoid open-ended 1–10 scales — people default to the middle and don't feel urgency

**Metaphors** — make the gap visceral
- Example for resume bullet quality: "Your resume reads like a job description, not an achievement record. Hiring managers don't care what your job was — they care what you did in it."
- Build contrasts that land in the gut, not just the head

**Questions** — ask them to self-assess across the three levers
1. "If your resume was actually working, what would be different right now?" *(Results)*
2. "How many roles have you applied to where you were qualified but never heard back?" *(Reality)*
3. "What specifically do you think is in the way?" *(Roadblocks)*

Questions don't need to be stacked back-to-back. Load one into any piece of content and let it do the work.

---

### 3. Product Placement

Embed a natural reference to the relevant offer inside the content before the CTA hits. The reader should encounter the offer mid-content as context, not at the end as a pitch.

**Rule:** Never interrupt content to sell. Find a genuine teaching tie-in.

**CTA-specific placement guidance:**

**Comment RESUME → Impact Bullet Builder**
Place in any post teaching a resume concept (bullet writing, formatting, what to include or cut). Mid-post, use a real before/after bullet example and attribute it to the Impact Bullet Builder by name. Don't explain what the IBB is — just use it as the source of the example. The reader sees the output, gets curious, and the CTA feels like a logical next step.

> *"Here's a bullet I rewrote using the Impact Bullet Builder — [before] → [after]. That's the difference between getting filtered out and getting a callback."*

**Comment COACH → Full Coaching Program**
Place in any post featuring a client result (Karen, Sarah, Jonah, AJ, Gavin). Mid-post, briefly reference that the result came from the 1-on-1 coaching process — not as a pitch, just as context for how the result happened. One sentence. Then continue teaching.

> *"Sarah didn't figure this out alone — we worked through it together in her first coaching session, and she had a $106K offer from Ford within weeks."*

**launchtolead.io/coaching → Full Offer**
Place in posts where the teaching is outcome-heavy (interviewing, offer negotiation, the full job search process). Mid-post, reference what the full coaching engagement covers — not as a list, just a passing mention that frames the scope.

> *"This is one piece of what we cover in the full coaching program — resume, interview prep, and the full search strategy, start to offer."*

**The Marjorie Rule:** Make the next step specific and vivid enough that the reader can picture themselves doing it before you ask. Specificity creates imagination. Imagination drives action.

---

### 4. The Easy Next Yes (The Invitation)

The CTA is an invitation, not a close. Match it to where the reader is in their awareness.

**One CTA or CTE per post — no exceptions.** Do not stack a funnel CTA (Comment RESUME/COACH) together with a separate engagement ask (Follow, Share) in the same post. Pick the single most important ask for that piece of content.

| Temperature | Who They Are | CTA |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold** — just found you, skeptical | Scrolling past, no prior exposure | Comment **RESUME** → get the Impact Bullet Builder guide (free, low friction) |
| **Warm** — engaged, curious, following | Consuming content, seeing results, considering | Comment **COACH** → get info on the full 1-on-1 coaching program |
| **Hot** — ready, comparing options | Has seen enough, wants to commit | **launchtolead.io/coaching** → see the full offer and apply |

- Never pitch the coaching program to a cold audience
- Never send a hot audience to a lead magnet
- The pitch tone: *"Hey, want to come to dinner? Cool if not."* — zero pressure, clear next step
- A pure engagement ask (Follow for more, Share with a friend) can substitute for a funnel CTA on posts not designed to convert — but never both in the same post

---

### 5. Content Planning

Before writing any piece of content, work through these three questions:

1. **What do I teach?** — What's the most useful thing for a Recent Grad on this topic? What's the old/new shift for this specific post?
2. **How do I make it engaging?** — What question, self-scoring exercise, or example gets them active, not passive?
3. **Where does it sell?** — Deliberately identify: where does the gap-stretching go? Where does the product placement go? What's the CTA and does it match audience temperature?

Step 3 is not improvised. If you don't plan it, it gets skipped.

Use **The Brandwidth Card** (below) as the physical template for this planning — one filled card per post.

## The Content Recipe (Formatting Layer)

Once the 5-Part strategy is decided, draft the post in this literal structure. This is what fills the sections above into an actual piece of content.

**Hook → Stack → Payoff → Invite**

- **Hook** — stops the scroll. This is where the old/new line-in-sand teaser or a Viral Hook template (see Appendix) lives. Timing: 3–5 sec on video, 1–2 slides on carousel, first 1–2 sentences in email.
- **Stack** — the meat of the content: the gap-stretching and teaching happen here. Built as one of three formats:
  - **Story** — something that happened to you, a friend, or a client (preferred default)
  - **List** — "3 things you need to look out for" (doesn't need to be in order; 3 is the standard count, 5 max)
  - **Steps** — numbered, sequential 1-2-3
- **Payoff** — the single most important idea, the pivot, or the action to take. This is where Product Placement naturally lands.
- **Invite** — the CTA or CTE (see Easy Next Yes above — one only).

**Format-specific timing:**
- **Email:** Hook = first 1–2 sentences; Stack = bulk of body; Payoff = short block near end; Invite = closing line
- **Reel/video:** Hook 3–5 sec, then Stack, Payoff, Invite as sequential beats
- **Carousel:** Hook = first 2 slides, Stack = several slides, Payoff = 1 slide, Invite = 1 slide

## Content Angles

Every post should rotate through one of these five angles. **All five still require the mandatory old/new contrast from Section 1** — the angle changes what the post leads with, not whether the contrast exists.

| Angle | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Agitates the pain directly — no solution yet, just makes them feel seen |
| **Proof** | Tells a client result story (see Proof Points) |
| **Philosophy** | Old way vs. new way framing, front and center |
| **Plan** | "Here's how I do it" — tactical, actionable |
| **PSA** | Low-pressure announcement — "I'm doing this, want in?" |

## Voice Rules

### DO
- Write as an engineer, not as HR or a recruiter
- Use specific numbers and concrete examples
- Reference real client outcomes by name (see Proof Points below)
- Be direct about the problem and the cost of inaction (~$7K/month in missed salary)
- Use "you" — address the reader directly
- Use the Hormozi Value Equation to stress-test persuasive copy: maximize Dream Outcome + Perceived Likelihood, minimize Time Delay + Effort

### DON'T
- Use hype language or exclamation marks in professional content
- Promise guaranteed job placement — we guarantee the deliverables and the process, not the job
- Use generic coaching buzzwords: "game-changer," "unlock your potential," "secret sauce," "hack," "crush it," "level up," "cheat sheet"
- Sound like a recruiter or HR professional
- Teach in general terms — always make it engineering-specific and Recent Grad-specific

## Format-Specific Notes

### LinkedIn Posts
- Professional but personal — share real coaching insights, tag real results
- Hook-first — the first line decides whether they read the rest
- One core idea per post, one CTA (or CTE) per post

### LinkedIn Document Ads
- Structure: Audience callout → Pain hook → Solution tease → Lead magnet offer
- Intro text: 2–3 sentences max — feed scanning, not reading

### TikTok / Instagram
- Hook-first, short-form, engineering career tips
- Hook must land in the first 2 seconds

### Landing Pages
- Above the fold (mobile): Headline + before/after example + 2 testimonials + form — all visible without scrolling
- Below the fold: Reinforcement only — IBB cover image, what's included, more social proof, FAQ
- Form fields: First name + Email only

## Appendix B: The Magic Model (Internal Content-Pillar Map — Not Client-Facing)

**Internal use only.** These mission names are generic content-planning labels, not branded deliverables. Never use these terms in client-facing content — always use the exact Branded Method Names (see table above) when referencing actual program deliverables.

**Goal (center):** Land Your 1st Engineering Job

Three obstacle → milestone pairs, each cleared by a set of missions:

1. **Obstacle:** Invisible Candidate → **Milestone:** Interviews Scheduled
   - Mission A: Sharpen Resume *(maps to: The Impact Bullet Builder)*
   - Mission B: Target Roles
   - Bonus mission: Build Network

2. **Obstacle:** Empty Toolbox → **Milestone:** Toolbox Stocked
   - Mission A: Build Storybank *(maps to: The Master Story Vault, The Competency Story Map)*
   - Mission B: Map Skills
   - Bonus mission: Prep Interview Aids

3. **Obstacle:** Interview Panic → **Milestone:** Interview Ready
   - Mission A: Plan Strategy *(maps to: The Power Pause, The Numbered Roadmap, The Interview Cheat Sheet)*
   - Mission B: Run Mocks
   - Bonus mission: Navigate Post-Interviews

### Using the Magic Model to Generate Content Ideas

The Magic Model is a combinatorial content generator, not a fixed script. Pull different slices depending on what you need to create:

| Combination | What It Produces | Example Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Yellow only** | Aspirational/vision content — paint the destination | PSA-angle post: "This is what landing your first eng job actually looks like" |
| **Yellow + 1 Red + 1 Green + its Missions** | A single complete mini-narrative: pain → outcome → how we get there | Full Problem→Philosophy→Plan post arc for one specific pain point |
| **Whole wheel, high level** | Overview/authority content — show the full system at once | "Here's the exact 3-part process that gets engineers hired" (framework reveal post) |
| **1 Red (obstacle) only** | Pure agitation — Problem-angle content, no solution given yet | "If you're sending 100 applications and hearing nothing back, here's why" |
| **1 Green (milestone) only** | Pure outcome/proof content — what it feels like to hit this checkpoint | "The moment your toolbox is actually stocked, this is what changes" |
| **1 Mission only** | Tactical Plan-angle content — teach one specific skill | A full post on just "Target Roles" or just "Run Mocks" |
| **1 Bonus mission only** | Surprise/differentiation content — signals depth beyond the obvious | Teaser content about post-interview navigation or interview aid prep, without revealing it's a bonus |
| **Obstacle → Milestone pair (no missions)** | Before/after contrast post | "Empty Toolbox → Toolbox Stocked: what actually has to change" |

When asked for content ideas, default to naming which Magic Model slice a given idea pulls from (e.g., "this one's a 1-Red post on Interview Panic") so the angle and old/new contrast are already implied by the selection.

## Per-Post Checklist (Quick Reference)

- [ ] Who is this for? (Confirm: Recent Grad)
- [ ] What Magic Model slice, if any, is this pulling from?
- [ ] What angle is this? (Problem / Proof / Philosophy / Plan / PSA)
- [ ] What is the **old way vs. new way** for this specific post? (mandatory)
- [ ] What is the **one action** I want them to take at the end?
- [ ] Where does the **gap-stretching** go? (model, metric, metaphor, or question)
- [ ] Where does the **product placement** go? (mid-content, natural tie-in)
- [ ] Does the **CTA/CTE match audience temperature**, and is it the only one in this post?
- [ ] Does every section **teach AND sell**, or am I just teaching?
- [ ] Have I used the reader's **specific language** — rejection emails, callbacks, $7K/month — not generic job search language?
- [ ] Have I checked Voice Rules for banned buzzwords, especially if drawing from a Viral Hook template?
