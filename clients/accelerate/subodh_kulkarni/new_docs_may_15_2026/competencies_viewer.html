01 Caribou FEE Build — Repeated Runoff Issues (Gage Wheel + Connector Indexing) ▶
Situation Caribou FEE build was almost complete and as a lead electrical QE I was pulled to address QIT repeated issues at Runoff. A couple of repeated issues were gage wheel not moving and indexing of an inline connector being difficult. The failure mode can cause non-functional system. Task As a lead I wanted to address these issues to reduce the DPM. Field failures because of these issues mean increase in DPM. Good thing here was the run off operator was diligent and vocal and kept recording his observations. Root cause analysis indicated that for gage wheel issue a lag in the signal caused initial delay and for indexing of connector, the bracket did not have an expected design slot to arrest the rotary motion. Actions I led the investigations by visiting Run off station, interviewing PV&V engineer and operators and also line operators. Reviewed prints and models, operator procedures and created an evidence package. I developed a plan and structure on how to proceed. Got cross functional team including senior engineers for the root cause analysis. I set up recurring meetings with engineers from Hydraulic, electrical, and Manufacturing departments and stated urgency to address the issues. Goals were set to resolve these issues before next production cycle or have a running change to include in current ECM. Got an agreement from all on it. Positive feedback from supervisors, Senior QEs on how I was directing/driving the actions and kept everyone on the topic/track and about my direct approach. Solutions were tested and implemented. Results Design change bracket for connector and modified the algorithms to successfully addressed the issues within 6 weeks. 2 defects per machine at the runoff were addressed within 6 weeks. Positive feedback from supervisors, Senior QEs on how I was directing/driving the actions. Solutions were tested and implemented. No more complaints from operators or on the line regarding these issues.
Lesson Learned
Challenge the cross functional team member to bring changes on the components that are not working well. Could have driven actions to change the connector design and help the design engineer to find a new connector. Over time we now have a new square connector that does not need indexing.
02 Multicoupler Connector Housing — Micro-Crack / Supplier Material Defect (China) ▶
Situation Multicoupler Connector Housing had a micro-crack, potential of leaking and functional impact. Task As a lead I wanted to put this issue to bed for good and reduce warranty associated with it. Actions Led the investigations gathering the team. Root cause analysis indicated that the material coming from a supplier in China had this issue because of their stamping stroke was longer and created stress in the material. Drove actions — supplier was requested to change the processes, geometry/design of the housing also changed. Containment and secondary inspection processes were implemented. Escalated the importance as it needed a huge capital investment at the supplier. This part is only specifically designed for John Deere. TE is hard to work with. Multiple follow-ups with the team and supplier. Found a single source of truth. Multiple meetings and follow ups with teams, assigned tasks and action items and finally solution implemented. Finally received a date and proof in terms of pictures of implemented solution and processes. Results Clean point/change date received. 6,000+ connectors were inspected by supplier. 2 full years of waiting time from day we knew the problem to complete implementation. Done 2 weeks back. Issue not expected to recur.
Lesson Learned
Ask for testing results beforehand and make sure they are included in the PPAP. Could have asked for X-Ray or material integration test earlier.
03 Grizzly Rice Machines — Loading Auger Grain Tank Wire Harness Damage ▶
Situation Loading Auger Grain tank wire harness getting damaged/worn out in Grizzly Rice machines. Task As a lead I wanted to put this issue to bed for good and reduce warranty associated with it. Actions Led the investigations gathering the team. Root cause analysis indicated that higher mass flow rate impacts and abrasive nature of rice wears the conduit faster. Two-fold solution — route the harness differently and use a steel conduit in the projected area to protect it. Deviation was written with planned solution. However, implementation was delayed because of supplier processes of requotes and agreements. Reviewed warranty data. Followed up with the OFP/CPE QEs, SM and supplier. Found a single source of truth. Looked for alternatives to get samples or rework the existing harnesses from PDC. Got the sample that from supplier were stuck in Mexican customs, made sure the machines will have them based on priority when they will go out of door. Multiple meetings and follow ups with teams, assigned tasks and action items and finally solution implemented. Results Date and clean point: 7/20/2023. GSN best shipping date.
Lesson Learned
Do not assume that material will sustain extreme conditions — ask for requirements and work for application review. Could have used experience of working in rice and asked for PV&V tests for abrasion and wear.

01 Caribou Frames — Gage Redesign + Wireless Digital Data Collection (JDAAT) ▶
Situation Was using a gage for Caribou Frames. It was functional but not designed as per metrological principles — used C clamps causing deformation during tightening and saddle on indicator was obstructing components. The dial gage was getting damaged because of repetitive and frequent use. Decision making for U1 was not easy if we needed to measure each frame during production. Task Needed to continue data collection as well as find ways to make the process simpler and easier in decision making to accept or reject the frame. Actions Worked with the tool maker to make flat-ended toggle clamps for holding the gage against the frame, which reduced deformation. Changed the shape of the saddle to a smaller and shorter round for stability and surface matching. To make data collection digital, worked with MicroRidge company to get the wireless data transmitter from gages and worked on connecting to JDAAT. Wrote the logic in order to make decision making easier for an inspector — instead of numerical number crunching, categorical decision making was made possible. Proof of concept established and handed over to OFP. Results Step toward automation — seed project. Reduced inefficiencies of data collection — doubled the speed of data collection, time was reduced. Made sure that data validation takes place. Subjectivity in decision making was theoretically nullified.
Lesson Learned
Make sure that the process you inherited from others is abiding by engineering principles. Could have helped the engineer to improve her processes first and not started work before the process was improved — it took a lot of time. Delegation could have been a smarter way.
02 PhD Research — SCN (Soybean Cyst Nematode) Geospatial Regression Models ▶
Situation Hired as a doctoral student. Project was to deal with Soybean Cyst Nematode infestation in fields that could cause 30–100% yield loss and losing millions of dollars. The issue is this is a soil-borne pathogen which is difficult to detect. Engineering solutions were needed. Task To research current work done on this, management practices, methods of addressing it and come up with a viable proposal of a solution to further the knowledge. Actions Extensive literature review. Talked with researchers nationally and internationally. Some work was done to understand spectral signatures of crop canopies and yields, to see if pathogens can be detected using spectroscopy and aerial images. Proposed that work was needed on understanding the spatial structure of pathogens, their spatial distribution in the field and how it impacts accuracy of predictive models. Collected data on two fields — one large field and one plot study — over three years. Spectral response of crops was the response variable; SCN populations, soil type/texture, environmental factors such as rain, temp, plant height etc. were independent variables. All attributed with GPS location. Involved multiple data collection campaigns with various spatial and temporal resolutions. Data mining, filtering, writing algorithms, tweaking existing algorithms for data mining — representative data set over 120 quadrants. Results Applying spatial structure, geospatial regression models improved the relationships. Accuracy improved three-fold. Root mean square error for yield prediction improved by 2.5x and spectral response variable models three times. This approach of using spatial regression was first time used for predicting impact of SCN infestation on spectral response — unique and original contribution towards map-based farming / site-specific crop management. Best practices: a.) control/adjust nematicide application in areas where needed most, b.) sow SCN-resistant cultivar in areas where infestation occurs.
Lesson Learned
Learning algorithms or computer coding for MATLAB would have helped. Lesson learned — evaluate whom you are working with and what are their motives.

01 Caribou FK Frame — 25% FPY → Fixture Shim Adjustment (50 ft Alignment) ▶
Situation Pulled to help SOP fabrication of Caribou FK Frame build, as pandemic restricted travel of engineers from India. There was an alignment issue between skid plate and cutter bar. The misalignment causes belt rubs, warranty issues, PIPs. First pass yield was around 25% only, needed rework of 3 hours. Inconvenient to the repairman as well. Need to build 8 frames a day for production targets. Task As lead QE, interested in solving the problem by monitoring the reject rate, collecting data on frames, and recording before the build was complete. Actions First studied the problem and monitored trends by collecting data using a gage. Sorted and segregated data based on frame sizes. Realized alignment existed on 50 ft frames more than other sizes (35, 40, 45). Started focused monitoring of the manufacturing process, collected data on 50+ frames specifically in a few weeks, identified and established hypothesis and trends. Based on geometry of the fixture and frame, and magnitude of misalignment, proposed a shim reduction of 0.5 mm from one side of the weld fixture. Evaluated that this maneuvering was risky during production or on weekdays. There was resistance and reluctance from MEs and DEs to make any changes to fixture and prints at this stage. Manager was supportive. Discussed with FEE side quality supervisor, ME Staff Engineer, and Managers — decided to adjust the fixture by removing 0.5 mm shim on Friday to minimize production impact. Frame was inspected on CMM on Saturday on urgent basis and found that it worked. Problem resolved. Results Alignment for rest of the frames was within 5 mm. For each frame, saved 3 hours of work on almost 90% of 50 ft frames. Only one or two frames needed repair attributed to operator adjusting the fixture. Saved rubbing of belts and field failures for customers and warranty issues. Created DTAC document for repair on frames already built and sent to dealers. Fortunately only a few frames were sent to dealers by the time solution was worked out. MEs, repairman, FEE supervisor, ME supervisor all were happy with the result.
Lesson Learned
What fixtures have been used and what data of their inspection shows — could have studied first. Given the chance would have been a part of the fixture qualification process.
02 FLP Harness — Twisted Cable Non-Conformance (S Series + X Series, ~100 Machines) ▶
Situation Forward-Looking Perception (FLP) is an automation feature on latest combines. Functional accuracy of harnesses for this feature (AXE105253) is critical. Harnesses are used to receive signals for controller from camera and there is a critical aspect of harness assembly — necessary to have twisted and jacketed cables at four different connections. This harness is used for S Series and X Series machines. If the harnesses are not twisted as per specifications, signals received will be low or attenuated and will cause intermittent controller functions. Signal-related issues are not detectable in SMART tests or end-of-line testing. A few functional issues were witnessed in the field identified as quality issues by supplier during LPB-2023. Supplier never built these types of harnesses before LPB. Harnesses supplied by MURAT in Turkey. Task Make sure that harnesses installed are good and issues are not seen after the machine leaves the factory. Good quality parts go to the customer. Actions Had checked similar harnesses from the supplier in warehouse before they came to factory. Checking harness on X Series line — product owner mentioned an operator efficiency-related issue. That led to investigating the topic and reviewing the harness for twist. Realized the harnesses were not twisted to specification — instead of 10 mm of untwist, the untwist was 30+ mm. Wanted the whole conduit to be removed. Had previously talked with the supplier rep in Waterloo on other topics and knew he was going to Chicago — immediately called him and told him to stop by the factory on the way. He came after a couple of hours. Pulled the harness apart — revealed that the untwist was more than 70 mm and it was the case on all connectors on the particular harness. Immediate escalation — called the line QE and ME, educated them on problem and severity, took the opinion of product owner and asked about impact of repairing vs. replacing harnesses on the go, directed line QE to write QNotes on machines built so far and a few more for the next couple of weeks. Documented the build issue. Wrote an email marked "Urgent and Important" to supplier contacts. Immediately set up a meeting with John Deere cross-functional team including leads, PMs, and SM for first thing Monday morning. Asked the supplier rep to talk with their folks urgently and change the manufacturing process over the weekend. This was done Friday afternoon at approximately 5 PM. On Monday, came in early — checked S Series harness and realized the same issue was there too. Held internal meeting, discussed the situation, provided the plan, assigned tasks to cross-functional team members, QNoted all machines to be built based on rate. Connected with warehouse and PDC to quarantine parts. Requested SM folks to get with supplier and ship corrected parts. Asked for identification on corrected part. Planned the rework strategy and estimated repair time — approximately 10 minutes for X Series, 20 minutes for S Series. Results Expedited corrected harness from supplier. Made their process better, educated them on processes and expectations, then continued to build machines on the go. Around 50 machines on S Series and X Series each needed replacement of harnesses. 20 hours of rework altogether. Saved potential spec updates of tens of thousands of dollars to the company.
Lesson Learned
Would have asked the design engineers the corner conditions and print requirements on the changes.
