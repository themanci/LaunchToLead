The Job Sourcing Strategy‚Ñ¢

The Problem

The Reality

Week 2: Manager talks to referral.

Week 3: Job posted for compliance.

Week 4: 400 strangers apply.

Week 6: Referral gets the job.

Networking (5 Methods)

The Warm Intro Protocol‚Ñ¢

The Problem

Cold applying puts you in a pile of 500. You are a commodity.

The Solution

Get someone inside the castle to lower the drawbridge.

The "Cold Call Fallacy":

Most students think networking means asking strangers for jobs.
The Shift: You are not asking for a job. You are a Researcher gathering data.

The 4-Step Process:

  1. Identify: Find 3-5 alumni at your Target Company.
  2. Outreach: Send a "Low-Friction" connection request.
  3. The Call: Conduct a 15-minute "Researcher" interview.
  4. The Pivot: Convert the relationship into a referral.

The Trojan Horse Effect:

When you build a human connection, this person becomes your advocate. If a job opens up, they aren't recommending a "faceless applicant"‚Äîthey are recommending you, a person they know and like.

  • ‚Ä¢ Differentiation: You stop being a PDF and start being a person.
  • ‚Ä¢ Signal of Intent: Taking the time to ask intelligent questions proves you are serious about their company, not just desperate for any job.

The Outreach Script:

"Hi [Name], I'm a senior ME student at [School]. I see you're working at [Company]‚ÄîI've been following their work on [Project] and would love to ask 2-3 questions about your experience there. No expectations, just looking for perspective from a fellow alum. Thanks, [Your Name]"

Why It Works:

  • ‚Ä¢ Tribalism: "Fellow alum" creates instant trust.
  • ‚Ä¢ Flattery: Asking for advice validates their expertise.
  • ‚Ä¢ Low Friction: "No expectations" removes the pressure to hire you.

The Networking Mindset Shift‚Ñ¢

The Problem

Students feel like beggars asking for favors. This "inferiority complex" makes them awkward.

The Cost

70-85% of jobs aren't posted publicly‚Äîfilled via networks. Online apps have a dismal ~2% success rate, while referrals boost odds 4x.

The Shift

You are not a beggar. You are an Asset in Training. As a fresh grad, you bring energy, new tech skills (e.g., AI tools), and low-risk curiosity. Companies crave that.

"Networking is just making friends with a purpose."

The 4 Mental Reframes:

1. Seek Advice, Not Favors

Old: "Please give me a job." (Taker)

New: "I value your perspective on [Topic]." (Learner)

Why It Works: People love to give advice. It strokes their ego and feels low-risk.

Example: Instead of "Refer me?", say "What's one skill that helped you early at [Company]?"

2. The Permission Script

Old: "I'm bothering them."

New: "I am giving them a chance to pay it forward."

The Reality: Most seniors want to help the next generation. You are giving them an easy way to do good.

Script: "I know you're busy, but as someone who's been where I am, your insight could shape my path."

3. The "Fellow Alum" Angle

Old: "I'm a stranger."

New: "We are part of the same tribe."

The Tactic: Always lead with the shared connection (School, Hometown, Hobby). It instantly lowers the "Stranger Danger" defense.

Hook: "Saw your post on sustainable design; that's my capstone focus." (Boosts response 50%+).

4. The Reciprocity Loop

Old: "I have nothing to offer."

New: "I'm starting a two-way street."

Why It Works: Offer value back (e.g., "I can share my notes on latest ML trends").

Hack: End chats with "How can I help you?"‚Äîflips the power dynamic.

The Golden Rule

If you ask for a job, you get advice. If you ask for advice, you get a job.

Data: Networking fills 85% of roles indirectly through these convos.

Activation Tip: Practice with low-stakes (e.g., profs) before alums. Track in a journal: "Ask | Response | Next Step."

The Referral Request Script‚Ñ¢

The Cost

Missing the ask means missing out on referrals (30-50% of hires). Referral hires happen 10 days faster (29 vs. 39 days). Awkward asks lead to vague "good luck" responses.

The Solution

The "80% Pivot Moment". Wait until 15-20 mins in (80% through) to build reciprocity. Never ask in the first 5 minutes.

The Pivot Script:

"This has been incredibly helpful. Based on what you've shared, [Company] sounds like exactly the kind of environment I'm looking for. I noticed there's an opening for [Role]‚Äîwould you be open to referring me? If not, I completely understand and would still love to apply online."

Why it works: The "If not" clause removes pressure, making them more likely to say yes.

Engineering Variation:

"Based on your insights on [Tech Stack/Project], I'm excited about contributing similarly. Would you be open to referring me for the [Role]? If not, no worries at all."

The "Soft Ask" Alternative:

"Do you have any advice on the best way to get my application noticed for [Role]?"

Most likely response: "Send me your resume, I'll put it in the system." (Happens ~40% of the time).

Post-Ask Handling:

If Yes:

"Amazing, thank you! I'll send my resume now‚Äîany tips for tailoring?"

If No/Hesitant:

"No worries at all‚Äîyour advice alone is gold. Mind if I connect on LinkedIn to stay in touch?"

The Follow-Up:

Always send a thank-you note within 24 hours.

Real World Result:

A CS new grad used this pivot in a 20-min alumni chat; got referred to a mid-size tech firm and landed the role in 3 weeks. The alum specifically mentioned the "out" clause made it feel natural, not pushy.

The Problem

The Method

Hack: Ask for "speed mentoring" events‚Äîquick intros without commitment.

4. The Salary Data Request

Ask: "Do you have salary outcomes data for last year's graduating class for my major?"

Why: Real data beats Glassdoor. In 2025, request breakdowns by industry (e.g., software vs. mech).

Ask: "What resources do you have for skill assessments or certifications aligned with 2025 employer trends?"

The Gap Explanation Scripts‚Ñ¢

The Reality

Gaps affect 30-40% of young professionals. 75% of recruiters view short gaps neutrally if explained well. Gaps under 6 months rarely need explanation.

Common Gap Types

Graduation delay

Personal/family reasons

Health issues

Couldn't find job

The Framework (1-2 Sentences)

1. Acknowledge

State the gap neutrally without excuses

2. Pivot to Value

3. Connect to Role
