## Dream Outcome (Maximize)

"Land a Top-Tier, High Salary Engineering Job without the imposter syndrome or the job application void."

### The Problems

#### Resume Problems
- They need to get a resume together that is strong and stands out
- Knowing what experience to list on the resume is not clear
- Knowing how to demonstrate skills and competencies on the resume is not clear
- Knowing how to make the resume stand out in a pile of 1000 is not easy and trivial
- They might not have a lot of internships or experience on their resume and only be able to pull from coursework mostly
- They might not have had any extracurricular activities to put on the resume and this might really feel like their resume is weak
- Their GPA isn't extremely high and imposter syndrome is strong and they feel like they really can't be competitive
- Catering a resume to fit a job posting takes time and is painful to do for every single job posting they want to apply for
- Translating experiences into resume bullets that shine is difficult
- Writing out a resume that can be used to apply for jobs in a domain where they didn't necessarily study is difficult to translate

#### Application Strategy Problems
- They need to apply to companies strategically and not just apply to 1000 companies
- Figuring out which companies to apply to and which companies are good ones to work for is hard
- Figuring out which companies that they are top candidates for is difficult
- Coming up with talking points and questions to ask a recruiter or hiring interview panel about their company can be tricky, what to ask to make yourself look curious but not cross any weird boundaries or come off as awkward

#### Career Fair / Recruiting Event Problems
- They need to attend a career fair or a recruiting event to meet potential employers and discover new opportunities
- Finding the time with class and other commitments can be hard
- Getting the confidence to go talk to recruiters can be intimidating and hard
- Having a good elevator pitch prepared that captures a recruiters attention and makes them memorable is hard
- Knowing what to focus on and highlight in that short interaction is hard
- Knowing what questions to ask to stand out and seem genuinely like a good candidate is hard

#### Phone Screen Problems
- They need to pass a phone screen potentially as a first step
- Knowing what to focus and stress on in the phone interview is hard
- Being able to convey excitement and passion in a phone call can be very hard
- Being prepared for general fit questions can be hard for someone who is an engineer and ready for questions asking about technical projects they worked on
- Building some rapport and bantering in a "general fit" phone screen can be hard for less social people (which engineers tend to be)
- Knowing what questions to ask to understand if this company is even a good fit for them and them for the company is hard

#### Interview Problems
- They need to pass one or multiple in person rounds of interviews that are usually behavioral based
- Preparing for interviews is hard
- Figuring out which competencies this company is looking for is hard
- Preparing for a technical interview can be hard as well
- Finding quality practice partners to interview practice with is very hard
- Finding people who can give really valuable feedback in a mock interview is hard
- Carving out the time to document interview examples beforehand is time consuming and hard
- Sticking to a schedule and doing mock interviews well in advance of the interview is hard to hold oneself accountable to
- Performing well in an interview with a lot of pressure and things riding on it can be very stressful and hard
- Knowing how to conduct oneself in the interview can be hard
- Knowing what questions to come prepared with for the interviewers if there is time at the end of the interview can be very hard and tricky
- Knowing how to reach out and send a thank you and what to highlight in the thank you and include can be tricky and hard

#### Offer & Onboarding Problems
- They need to be able to negotiate a salary or benefits when an offer is made
- Assessing the quality of an offer can be hard
- Negotiating a salary/benefits as a young engineer can be hard, it can be easy to accept any first offer
- Knowing what to do in the first 90 days as a new engineer can be hard to make a good impression and hit the ground running to impress the new employer and manager
