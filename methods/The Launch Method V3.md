**The Sniper Approach:**
Instead of applying everywhere, go *deep* on a focused list of **10-15 companies**.

**1. Know their PROBLEMS, not just products.**
- **The "10-K" Scan:** For public companies, read the "Risk Factors" section of their annual report. It tells you exactly what they're afraid of (e.g., "Supply chain reliance on single-source chips").
- **The "Forum" Dig:** Search the company name on technical forums (EEVblog, Stack Overflow, Reddit). What are their engineers complaining about? Is their API documentation terrible? Is their testing infrastructure fragile?
- **The "News" Filter:** Search `[Company Name] AND "contract" OR "partnership"`. Did they just win a government grant? That means they have money but need staff.
- **2025 Focus:** Emphasize AI/sustainability problemsâ€”companies like Boeing and Tesla prioritize these in hires.

**2. Map the org chart.**
- Use LinkedIn's "People" tab or TheOrg.com to identify 3-5 contacts (alumni, 2nd-degree connections)
- Note hierarchies for outreachâ€”start mid-level (senior engineers, managers), not C-suite
- For engineering roles, filter for "software dev manager" or "engineering lead"
- Find the hiring manager's name before you apply

**3. Engage BEFORE you ask.**
- **The Rule:** You are not allowed to ask for a referral until you have provided value first.
- **Comment:** Leave thoughtful technical comments on their LinkedIn posts (not just "Great post!")
- **Share:** Send them a relevant article: "Saw you're working on ADAS; this IEEE paper on sensor fusion just dropped and reminded me of your team's work."
- **Connect:** "I'm not looking for a job right now, just following your work on [Project X]."
- **Timing:** Engage 1-2 weeks before applying. In 2025, 85% of referrals start with digital engagement.

**4. Tailor EVERYTHING.**
- Customize resume and cover letter with keywords from the JD (use Jobscan for ATS matching)
- Align portfolio projects to their tech stack (e.g., Python for FAANG, MATLAB for aerospace)
- Include a follow-up cadence: nudge 1 week post-apply via LinkedIn
- Track everything in a pipeline spreadsheet

# 2. Networking (5 Methods)

### The Warm Intro Protocolâ„¢
**The Core Problem:** Cold applying puts you in a pile of 500. You are a commodity.
**The Solution:** Get someone *inside* the castle to lower the drawbridge.

**The "Cold Call Fallacy":**
Most students think networking means asking strangers for jobs. This feels gross and gets rejected.
**The Shift:** You are not asking for a job. You are a **Researcher** gathering data about the industry.

**The Trojan Horse Effect:**
When you build a human connection, this person becomes your advocate. If a job opens up, they aren't recommending a "faceless applicant"â€”they are recommending *you*, a person they know and like.
*   **Differentiation:** You stop being a PDF and start being a person.
*   **Signal of Intent:** Taking the time to ask intelligent questions proves you are serious about *their* company, not just desperate for *any* job. It separates you from the "spray and pray" crowd.

**The 4-Step Process:**
1.  **Identify:** Find 3-5 alumni from your school working at your Target Company (Tier 1/2). Use LinkedIn Alumni tool.
2.  **Outreach:** Send a "Low-Friction" connection request.
3.  **The Call:** Conduct a 15-minute "Researcher" interview.
4.  **The Pivot:** Convert the relationship into a referral.

**The Outreach Script:**
> "Hi [Name], I'm a senior ME student at [School]. I see you're working at [Company]â€”I've been following their work on [Project] and would love to ask 2-3 questions about your experience there. No expectations, just looking for perspective from a fellow alum. Thanks, [Your Name]"

**Why It Works:**
*   **Tribalism:** "Fellow alum" creates instant trust.
*   **Flattery:** Asking for advice validates their expertise.
*   **Low Friction:** "No expectations" removes the pressure to hire you.
